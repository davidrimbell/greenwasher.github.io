Uncovering Deceptive Practices: From Oil Companies to Tech Giants, the Fight Against Greenwashing Continues

Published: 2023-09-13

In today’s news, we uncover the deceptive practices and false advertising that continue to plague the world of sustainability. From oil and gas companies claiming to support the energy transition to tech giants accused of greenwashing, it’s clear that the fight against greenwashing is far from over.

Let’s start with Aquaterra Energy, a company specializing in oil and gas equipment and services. While they claim to be adapting their business to the energy transition, they are not de-emphasizing their traditional business in favor of renewable projects. Critics argue that this is a clear case of greenwashing, as the company continues to profit from fossil fuels while making token efforts towards sustainability.

Next, we turn our attention to Apple, a company that has been called out for its hypocritical stance on climate change. While they promote themselves as being carbon neutral and using 100% green energy, critics argue that their commitment to climate change is merely a marketing ploy. Apple’s use of Chinese sweatshops and misleading claims about renewable energy have raised questions about their true dedication to sustainability.

But it’s not just big corporations that are guilty of greenwashing. Baileys, the popular liqueur brand, has introduced a new addition to its portfolio: Baileys Chocolate Liqueur. While it may sound indulgent and delicious, critics argue that this is simply a ploy to capitalize on the growing demand for sustainable products. The launch is accompanied by a flashy marketing campaign, but it remains to be seen whether Baileys' claims of sustainability hold true.

On a more positive note, the U.S. construction industry is quietly embracing ESG principles, despite avoiding specific references to the term. The industry’s focus on decarbonization and sustainability aligns with global efforts and positions it for growth opportunities. This is a promising step forward, as the construction industry has a significant impact on carbon emissions and resource consumption.

In California, the state senate has approved a bill that will require companies with annual revenue over $1 billion to report their carbon emissions. This is a significant milestone, as it will be the first mandate of its kind. The bill reflects the growing trend of ESG reporting and may set a precedent for federal regulations in the future.

Finally, in Indonesia, the Green Digital Economy Platform (GDEP) has been launched to empower farmers with AI and technology. This platform aims to champion sustainability and advance the digital economy, leveraging technological advancements and global collaboration. By monitoring the impact on the digital economy and sustainability, the GDEP seeks to drive transformative innovations in agriculture and climate technology.

As we navigate the complex world of greenwashing, it’s important to remain vigilant and question the claims made by companies and organizations. By staying informed and holding businesses accountable, we can work towards a more sustainable future for ourselves and future generations.
