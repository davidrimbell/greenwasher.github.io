Corporate food systems grab should not get UN approval

The UNs annual food systems summit could be a significant step forward in the fight against hunger and climate change, according to two leading human rights activists who have called for the UN to take action to tackle disastrous consequences for food and livelihoods. The BBC s Simona Haley looks at what they say. But What is the threat to the food system - and what is it likely to be the biggest challenge for human right to food, writes Shalmali Gunter and Sofia Monsalve in an open Democracy conference in Rome on the issue of sustainable agriculture and the impact of the coronavirus pandemic on global crops, and how the world is affected by the effects of Covid-19 on food supplying plants and humans and its impact on human health, environment and life, in their latest assessment of how it is being treated by giant corporations that dominate the global food networks? They are calling for them to raise awareness of what happens to global health and environment, asks the BBC. Why does it mean that the event would be an opportunity to address the issues that have been thrown out of public attention in recent years? And what might it be like to help those who feed the planet?
