The Impact of Greenwashing: From Aviation to Fashion

Published: 2023-06-25

Sustainability and the fight against climate change continue to be hot topics in various industries around the world. From aviation to fashion, companies are under pressure to adopt more eco-friendly practices and reduce their carbon footprint. However, amidst these efforts, there is a growing concern about greenwashing – the deceptive practice of making false or misleading claims about a product or company’s environmental impact.

One industry that has been particularly scrutinized for greenwashing is aviation. At the recent Paris Air Show, sustainability was a major focus, with the aviation industry under pressure to reduce greenhouse gas emissions. However, going green in flying may be more expensive and time-consuming than initially anticipated. While many airlines have made commitments to use sustainable aviation fuel (SAF), the reality is that SAF currently accounts for just 0.1% of all jet fuel and is significantly more expensive than conventional fuel. Critics argue that airlines are making overly ambitious promises about their use of SAF, as the limited supply and infrastructure make it difficult to scale up production. Some airlines have even faced legal action and complaints for misleading consumers with claims of being carbon-neutral.

Another industry facing accusations of greenwashing is the corporate world. Many companies in America are embracing the woke agenda, prioritizing extreme climate change projections, diversity and inclusion programs, and diversity in governing boards. While these practices may seem noble, they do not necessarily lead to increased efficiency and profitability. Examples of companies suffering from this approach include Anheuser-Busch, Target, and the LA Dodgers. To combat this, consumers are encouraged to boycott companies that practice this form of cowardice and invest in funds that do not promote ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) policies.

In the Philippines, the government is urging the private sector to support its efforts in building a sustainable country. The Department of Environment and Natural Resources (DENR) Secretary Maria Antonia Yulo-Loyzaga emphasized the importance of preserving the environment and addressing unsustainable consumption and practices, particularly in relation to plastic waste. The DENR is seeking partnerships and innovations to combat water and air pollution, and collaboration between the government and the private sector is crucial to achieving environmental protection and sustainability goals.

On a more positive note, some companies are genuinely making efforts to be more sustainable. DP World, for example, is accelerating its adoption of renewable energy at the Port of Santos in Brazil as part of its global strategy to achieve carbon neutrality by 2040. The company is investing in electrifying its container handling cranes, reducing diesel consumption by up to 60%. Additionally, DP World is implementing various environmental initiatives, including waste management and conservation projects.

Chinese fashion companies are also turning to environmental, social, and corporate governance (ESG) as a key strategy for faster development. With weakening foreign demand and a sluggish economy, companies are investing more in ESG governance to obtain longer-term returns. ESG disclosure can help reduce financing costs and impact consumer purchasing behavior. Chinese companies are urged to align with international standards as new ESG disclosure guidelines are expected to be issued.

While there are companies genuinely committed to sustainability, it is important for consumers to be aware of greenwashing and not be deceived by false advertising. It is crucial to do thorough research and support companies that are transparent and genuinely committed to sustainable practices. By holding companies accountable and making informed choices, we can contribute to a greener and more sustainable future for ourselves and future generations.
