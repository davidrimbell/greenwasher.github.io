Dauphin County company resets management team as president retires

Phoenix Contact USA, a Lower Swatara Township-based company, has announced a new management structure and leadership team as it transitions. Jack Nehlig, the company s president for 23 years, is retiring on December 31. Heath Scoggin has been promoted to president of BA Device Connectors, leading Phoenix Contact Development and Manufacturing legal entity, Process Management, and Quality. Kevin Zak has been appointed president of BA Industrial Components and Electronics, overseeing the U.S. Sales Subsidiary legal entity, Logistics, Government Relations, and ESG. Davis Mathews has been promoted to President of BA Industry Management and Automation, managing the Phoenix Contact Holdings legal entity, Human Relations, Digitalization, IT, Purchasing, and Facilities. Kyle Bordner has been promoted to vice president of finance and risk management, replacing David Russo, who retired in May. Scoggin, Zak, Mathews, and Bordner have been named members of both the Phoenix Contact USA executive board and the management board. The new management board also includes Marian Roldan, senior vice president of human relations, and Doug Ferguson, senior vice president of Americas Operations Services. The changes took effect on September 1. The USA Management Board acknowledges Jack Nehlig s leadership, mentorship, and friendship, and they aim to ensure a smooth leadership transition, align with the corporate management team, and enhance the USA s market influence in future product and solution development. Phoenix Contact, founded in 1923, has 22,000 employees in over 100 countries and employs more than 1,100 people across the U.S. The company creates products to connect, distribute, and control power and data flows.
