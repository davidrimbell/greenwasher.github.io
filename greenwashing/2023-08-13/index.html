Authors Walk Out of Edinburgh Book Festival Over Fossil Fuel Sponsorship

Published: 2023-08-13

Over 50 authors and event chairs have staged a walkout from the Edinburgh International Book Festival in protest against its links to fossil fuel companies. The authors, including renowned writers Ali Smith and Zadie Smith, have signed an open letter demanding alternative sponsors if the festival’s main sponsor, investment management firm Baillie Gifford, does not divest from the fossil fuel industry.

The authors accuse Baillie Gifford of profiting from the climate crisis, claiming that the firm has invested up to £5 billion in fossil fuel corporations. They argue that supporting the festival through such sponsorship puts corporate financial support for the arts at risk. Climate activist Greta Thunberg recently withdrew from the festival, accusing Baillie Gifford of “greenwashing” through its sponsorship of cultural events.

This protest highlights the growing concern over greenwashing, a deceptive practice in which companies misrepresent their environmental impact or sustainability efforts. Greenwashing can take many forms, including false advertising, misleading claims, and dishonest marketing tactics. It is a form of deception that undermines genuine efforts to address climate change and protect the environment.

In another news, a recent internal briefing note from the Water Corporation revealed that Alcoa’s mining operations pose a significant risk to water quality and supply. The note also highlighted the lack of effective regulation due to outdated state agreements. Alcoa’s claims of not negatively impacting public drinking water supply and rehabilitating cleared areas were found to be misleading. This case sheds light on the issue of sustainability washing, where companies falsely portray themselves as environmentally responsible while engaging in harmful practices.

These examples of greenwashing and sustainability washing demonstrate the need for greater transparency and accountability in corporate practices. It is crucial for consumers to be aware of these deceptive tactics and to support companies that genuinely prioritize sustainability and environmental responsibility.

On a more positive note, Clint Flores, head of the ESG Department at Bank of Valletta, emphasizes the competitive advantage that a strong commitment to environmental, social, and governance (ESG) practices can give hospitality companies. Embracing sustainability and setting science-based targets to reduce carbon emissions can not only benefit the environment but also contribute to the long-term success of businesses.

The Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) region is also focusing on sustainability in the logistics sector as part of efforts to reduce greenhouse gas emissions. Governments in the region are setting targets for emissions reduction and adopting green initiatives, such as alternate transportation fuel and low-emission maritime vessels. This shift towards sustainable supply chain practices will play a major role in reducing emissions and reshaping the global business environment.

In conclusion, the fight against greenwashing and sustainability washing is ongoing. It is essential for consumers to be vigilant and support companies that prioritize genuine sustainability efforts. By holding companies accountable and demanding transparency, we can contribute to a greener and more sustainable future for generations to come.
