Kellogg's Suspends Palm Oil Purchases from Astra Agro Lestari Amid Allegations of Land Grabbing and Environmental Degradation

Eaton, a global power management company, has recently released its 2022 Sustainability Report, outlining its commitment to addressing environmental, social, and governance (ESG) issues. The company has set ambitious targets to reduce carbon emissions, achieve carbon neutrality, and certify manufacturing sites as zero waste to landfill. Eaton is also focused on enhancing employee safety, development, and engagement, and providing transparency on its progress towards ESG goals. By creating sustainable solutions, reducing its footprint, engaging employees and communities, and conducting business ethically and transparently, Eaton is leading the way in corporate sustainability.

Kellogg’s, a major consumer goods brand, has joined other companies in suspending its purchases of palm oil from Indonesian company Astra Agro Lestari (AAL) due to allegations of land grabbing and environmental degradation. A report published in 2022 accused three AAL subsidiaries of engaging in these practices, as well as criminal persecution of environmental and human rights defenders. While AAL has denied the allegations and launched an independent investigation, activists argue that the burden of proof should be on the company, not the local communities affected. They are calling on other companies and investors to take a stand against AAL and support sustainable practices.

The Australian Competition and Consumer Commission (ACCC) has released draft guidance on how businesses can avoid greenwashing, which refers to misleading environmental or sustainability claims. The guidance includes eight key principles for businesses to follow, such as providing accurate and truthful claims, using clear and easily comprehensible language, and being open about sustainability transition plans. The ACCC conducted an “internet sweep” in 2022 and found that 57% of Australian companies made concerning environmental claims. The ACCC is prioritizing enforcement action against greenwashing and is seeking feedback on the guidance until September 15, 2023. Greenwashing is also a growing concern in the EU.

BDO Unibank Inc. has been recognized for its Sustainable Finance Initiative at the 2023 Asian Banking & Finance Wholesale Banking Awards. The bank has demonstrated its commitment to sustainable finance by financing renewable energy projects, forging partnerships with international financial institutions, and integrating Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) principles into funding projects. BDO has financed 55 renewable energy projects since 2010, totaling 2,230 megawatts of installed capacity. The bank continues to expand its sustainable finance initiatives to support the country’s greenhouse gas reduction goals.

VMware, a technology solutions company, has been focused on sustainability for over 25 years. Their virtualization technology has helped companies reduce hardware and energy consumption, leading to smaller carbon footprints. Sustainability is a core component of VMware’s business, and they have adopted the 2030 Agenda to guide their strategy for decarbonizing digital infrastructures. They consider energy consumption, carbon emissions, electronic waste, and water usage throughout the life cycle of their products and services. VMware is committed to co-innovation and working with partners and customers to achieve net-zero goals. They are also integrating sustainability into emerging technologies like artificial intelligence and machine learning.

The fashion industry’s environmental impact, particularly in fast fashion, is largely unknown due to inaccurate and unreliable methods of assessing it. Metrics, certification schemes, and labels used to mark the environmental consequences of clothing production have been accused of greenwashing. Without accurate assessments, consumers are unable to make informed decisions. A transparent and collective effort across the industry is needed to accurately measure and reduce fashion’s environmental footprint.

These are just a few examples of the ongoing efforts and challenges in the world of sustainability. It is crucial for consumers to be aware of greenwashing and to support companies that are genuinely committed to sustainable practices. By staying informed and making conscious choices, we can all contribute to a greener and more sustainable future for generations to come.
