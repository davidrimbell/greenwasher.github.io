Sustainability vs affordability : how can brands and retailers strike the right balance ?

Consumers are increasingly concerned about sustainable behaviours and their attitudes to the environment. But how can brands and retailers strike the right balance between affordability, sustainability and the impact on shoppers lives? The Grocer Vision series has revealed how they can tackle a growing crisis in the UK and Ireland. How can the BBC. But How is it going to be able to explain the challenges being handled by consumers in 2023? Why is the industry struggling to make it harder for them to take advantage of the cost-of-living crises, and what could the worlds biggest retailer become responsible for savings - and how does it work to reduce the risk of climate change? What would be the best way to stop those buying these products and services without having to do so? And why are the brands getting the same advice on the future of retailing and whether it is possible to help them cope with the global economy when the coronavirus pandemic is coming into force? Here is an expert panel looking into how brands can help avoid rising costs of living and food waste, as well as how should the retail industry take action to protect themselves from environmental concerns? Is it possible for brands to keep up with renewable packaging and goods that have been driven by an increase in supply and quality of food and drinks for the past 12 months, asks Peter McDonell, who looks at how it can be tackled?
