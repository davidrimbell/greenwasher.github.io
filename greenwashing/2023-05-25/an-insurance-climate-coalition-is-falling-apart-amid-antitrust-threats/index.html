An Insurance Climate Coalition Is Falling Apart Amid Antitrust Threats

The worlds biggest insurer, Munich Re, has announced that it is withdrawn from the Net-zero Asset Owner Alliance (Nzia). The group has said it would remain committed to their climate ambitions within the alliance, but says it will not pursue them individually outside of the Nzia framework, it has emerged. (). It is to be the first major fossil fuel insurance firm to quit the group, as the US Supreme Court has ruled out a row over the impact of antitrust threats to the insurers environmental agency, the net- zero estate owners Alliance, and could become the world s second largest insurance group to take part in the global warming initiative, NSIA, in which they are being accused of pushing up decarbonisation targets and imposing tariffs on carbon intensive industries, despite an increase in anti-corruption claims that the firm is trying to tackle the risks of rising emissions and de carbonisation in its efforts to reduce the number of greenhouse gases and energy costs, with the loss of more than half of its founding members, who have filed complaints about the future of carbon defence against the company, saying it may be illegal.
