BoardPAC Appointed Knowledge Partner of Board Stewardship Inc., a New Magazine Founded by Times of India Group

Published: 2023-05-22

BoardPAC, the leading board meeting automation company, has been appointed as the knowledge partner of Board Stewardship Inc., a new magazine founded by Times of India Group. This partnership aims to provide valuable resources and industry insights to board members of leading companies in India. BoardPAC is excited to share their expertise and congratulate Board Stewardship Inc. on this achievement. The magazine covers important topics such as board compliance news, ESG, sustainability, and cyber security. BoardPAC is a trusted platform used by top-ranked clients across 40+ countries.

The Associated Press (AP) has been accused of promoting climate change alarmism and pushing a woke environmental agenda in its reporting. It was revealed that AP received an $8 million grant from left-wing nonprofit organizations, including the Rockefeller Foundation and the Walton Family Foundation, to expand its climate coverage. Critics argue that AP’s reporting is biased and lacks objectivity, serving as a mouthpiece for its donors. The organization has been accused of using leftist buzzwords and framing climate change as an apocalyptic threat. The funding from these organizations raises concerns about conflicts of interest and the independence of AP’s journalism.

Indigo Ag, a sustainability partner in the agriculture industry, is helping consumer goods companies measure and reduce their Scope 3 emissions and water use through their agricultural supply chain. Through their Market+ Source program, Indigo connects companies to sustainably grown crops within their existing supply chain. They have successfully delivered over 10 million bushels of sustainably grown grains, reducing emissions by over 15,000 metric tons and saving 6.7 billion gallons of water. Indigo has also delivered sustainably grown cotton to brands like The North Face. Their proprietary measurement, reporting, and verification capabilities ensure the environmental benefits of their program.

Mining companies are now being evaluated by their buyers based on the type of energy they use in their operations, according to the Offshore Mining Chamber of the Philippines. Miners are required to disclose their energy sources, and if they are not clean or green, buyers may refuse to do business with them. This emerging norm is driven by the need to combat the global climate emergency. Demand for clean energy in supply chains is largely driven by companies' performance on environmental, social, and governance criteria. However, some miners in the Philippines face challenges in accessing renewable power.

The Makati Commerce Tower in the Philippines is the country’s first and only LEED Platinum version 4.0 office building. Developed by Empresas Diesel Development, it offers 25 floors of office space, retail establishments, a food court, and parking levels. The building features sustainable and wellness-focused elements such as touchless technology, energy-saving features, electric vehicle parking, and bicycle parking with shower facilities. The Makati Commerce Tower’s LEED certification represents a commitment to making the world a better place and influencing others to do better in terms of sustainability. Landlords are now highlighting these features to attract tenants.

Latham & Watkins recently hosted a panel discussion on greenwashing during London International Disputes Week. The panel discussed key drivers of greenwashing complaints, challenges faced by organizations in defining and explaining their sustainability plans, and global litigation trends. The panel also highlighted the importance of aligning external messaging with corporate practice and evolving regulatory expectations. They discussed the enforcement and regulatory risks associated with greenwashing, including the role of regulators such as the Advertising Standards Authority and the Competition and Market Authority. The panel also noted the growing importance of greenwashing litigation and the complexity of these cases. They concluded by discussing potential future developments in greenwashing litigation, including the role of human rights and the need for consensus on key concepts.

ASIC, the Australian Securities and Investments Commission, may start scrutinizing popular ETFs that hold stocks deemed unethical by leading investment managers. One such stock is Nvidia Corporation, a favorite of ethical ETFs, but around 45% of its core product, graphic processing units (GPUs), are used for video gaming, which is considered less impactful. Additionally, Nvidia GPUs are used in cryptocurrency mining and AI development, which raises questions about their ESG profile. The article highlights the challenges of defining and implementing ESG criteria in investment strategies and the need for authenticity and accountability in impact investing. It also mentions examples of passive funds with ethical labels that contain questionable investments, emphasizing the rise of greenwashing. ASIC is increasing its enforcement efforts to address greenwashing and ensure funds are true to their labels.

In 2021, 145 country leaders pledged to halt and reverse forest loss by 2030. However, the demand for forest carbon credits has leveled off in 2022 due to uncertainty about the rules and concerns about the quality of credits. The voluntary carbon market, where companies purchase credits to fulfill voluntary commitments, lacks regulations and independent frameworks. To address this, a new guide called the Tropical Forest Credit Integrity Guide has been created to help companies develop a portfolio of high-quality tropical forest carbon credits. Companies can play a crucial role in protecting tropical forests and signaling demand for carbon credits.
