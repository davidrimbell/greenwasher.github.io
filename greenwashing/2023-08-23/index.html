"Carlisle Companies Releases 2022 Sustainability Report, Demonstrating Commitment to Sustainable Practices"

Carlisle Companies Incorporated, a leading supplier of building envelope products and solutions, has released its 2022 Sustainability Report, showcasing its commitment to socially and fiscally responsible practices. The report highlights the company’s efforts to produce energy-efficient building products, reduce emissions, and manage waste. With a goal of achieving net-zero greenhouse gas emissions by 2050, Carlisle is taking significant steps towards a more sustainable future.

In the realm of international business, companies face the challenge of staying compliant with anti-corruption regulations. To tackle this issue, there are three key steps that businesses can follow. First, it is crucial to establish a company culture that prioritizes anti-corruption regulation from top to bottom. By ensuring that all employees understand and uphold these regulations, businesses can protect themselves from corruption. Second, understanding the local business culture in the jurisdictions where business is conducted is essential. This includes researching customs, norms, and legal systems to ensure compliance. Finally, having boots on the ground intelligence is vital. Thorough research and understanding of the local market can help businesses navigate potential risks and operate ethically.

Ken LaRoe, CEO of Climate First Bank, remains committed to promoting environmental, social, and governance (ESG) values despite the current anti-ESG movement. The bank has already processed $134 million in applications for solar loans, with $66 million on the books and 1,300 loans issued. LaRoe plans to expand the solar lending platform nationwide and collaborate with other banks to provide the necessary technology and expertise. The bank’s fintech subsidiary, One Ethos, will serve as a lender and technology provider, offering an app that allows consumers to track their environmental impact and make values-aligned financial decisions. LaRoe is even open to including animal welfare considerations in the app in the future.

Mercado Global and the Young Professionals of the Americas are joining forces to host an event focused on sustainability in business strategy and product design. The event will feature an industry panel discussion, showcasing climate- and socially-conscious fashion and design, as well as sustainably sourced snacks. Attendees will have the opportunity to purchase Mercado Global products at a discount and sample sustainably-sourced food and beverages from various brands.

The Department of Energy (DOE) has announced plans to launch direct air capture (DAC) hubs in Texas and Louisiana, aiming to capture over 2 million metric tons of CO2 emissions each year. However, environmental advocates have expressed skepticism, arguing that this move could be a form of greenwashing, allowing fossil fuel companies to continue their operations. Concerns have been raised about the efficacy of DAC technology and the potential consequences of storing captured carbon underground. Nevertheless, some groups see this as an opportunity to scale up carbon removal technology and reduce carbon levels.

The e-commerce sector faces a significant challenge in managing Scope 3 emissions, which account for a substantial portion of total emissions in the value chain. Last-mile delivery alone contributes to half of all transport emissions in the global e-commerce sector. To address this issue, brands can prioritize visibility and transparency in their logistics by choosing the right partners and implementing data-led solutions to monitor and reduce emissions. While there is currently no globally aligned reporting standard for Scope 3 emissions, legislation is being rolled out in Europe to make sustainability reporting and auditing mandatory. By investing in sustainable packaging, offering sustainable delivery options, and demanding transparency from partners, brands can meet consumer expectations and reduce emissions.

Rayonier Inc. has also released its 2022 Sustainability Report, emphasizing its commitment to long-term sustainability and the well-being of stakeholders. The report highlights the integration of environmental, social, and governance factors into decision-making processes and promotes two-way communication to foster understanding of the benefits of working forests and sustainability initiatives.
