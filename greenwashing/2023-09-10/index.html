The Importance of Sustainability in Middle Eastern Supply Chains: Addressing Greenwashing and Driving Positive Change

Published: 2023-09-10

Manufacturers in the Middle East are facing increasing pressure to develop sustainable and digitally connected supply chains. The COVID-19 pandemic has highlighted the importance of supply chain management, with issues such as risk management and near-shoring becoming top priorities. But it’s not just about profitability and meeting the demands of a circular economy. Sustainability is now a key focus.

Greenhouse gas emissions and operating costs attributed to supply chains have become significant concerns. To support long-term growth and profitability, manufacturers must embed sustainability into their supply chains. This means proving that products are sustainably sourced and addressing environmental, social, and governance (ESG) pledges.

Enterprise resource planning (ERP) solutions play a crucial role in achieving these goals. They provide visibility, reporting, and ensure compliance with environmental standards. With the help of ERP solutions, manufacturers can make informed decisions about sustainability and drive positive change.

But it’s not just manufacturers who are taking steps towards sustainability. The banking sector is also making efforts to reduce its environmental impact. Bank of Valletta, for example, has reopened its Naxxar branch after extensive refurbishment works. The renovation incorporates environmentally friendly concepts, such as sustainability and reduced emissions. By putting the customer at the center of operations, the bank aims to provide the highest level of service in a modern and eco-friendly environment.

AVEVA, a global leader in industrial software, is also making strides in sustainability. The company has achieved its greenhouse gas emissions targets for 2025 by reducing emissions across its operations and business travel. AVEVA has also invested in software solutions that help customers in their sustainability efforts. The company’s sustainability report highlights its commitment to reducing its environmental footprint and enhancing its sustainability handprint.

While these efforts are commendable, it’s important to remain vigilant against greenwashing. Greenwashing refers to the deceptive practice of making misleading or unsubstantiated claims about the environmental benefits of a product, service, or company. It is a form of deception, misrepresentation, and dishonesty that can mislead consumers and undermine genuine sustainability efforts.

Green marketing, eco-washing, sustainability washing, and whitewashing are all terms associated with greenwashing. False advertising is a common tactic used by companies to make their products appear more sustainable than they actually are. It is essential for consumers to be aware of these tactics and to critically evaluate the environmental claims made by companies.

As concerned individuals, we must hold companies accountable for their sustainability claims. We can do this by researching and verifying the information provided, looking for third-party certifications, and supporting companies that have a genuine commitment to sustainability.

In conclusion, sustainability is no longer just a buzzword. It is a crucial aspect of supply chain management and business operations. Manufacturers and companies across various sectors are taking steps towards sustainability, but we must remain vigilant against greenwashing. By staying informed and holding companies accountable, we can contribute to a more sustainable future for ourselves and future generations.
