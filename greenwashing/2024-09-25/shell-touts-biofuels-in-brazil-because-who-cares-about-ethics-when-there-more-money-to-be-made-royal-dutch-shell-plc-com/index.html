Shell Touts Biofuels in Brazil Because Who Cares About Ethics When There More Money to Be Made ? – Royal Dutch Shell Plc . com

Shell, an oil giant notorious for environmental destruction, employee exploitation, and moral bankruptcy, is now promoting its biofuels project in Brazil. The company s CEO, Wael Sawan, announced a commitment to second-generation ethanol from sugarcane bagasse, claiming it could yield 50% more ethanol on the same land. However, this move is seen as greenwashing, as Shell continues to invest in deepwater oil and gas projects with Petrobras. Shell s history is marred by controversies, including its collaboration with the Nazis, testing carcinogenic chemicals on employees, and a North Sea platform scandal where safety standards were compromised. The company has also been accused of spying on environmentalists like Greenpeace. Shell s investor, BlackRock, is implicated in the company s shady dealings worldwide. Despite the numerous scandals, John Donovan, the owner of royaldutchshellplc.com, has not faced any libel suits for exposing Shell s misdeeds. Shell has partnered with Cosan to establish 2G ethanol plants in Brazil, but it remains heavily invested in Brazil s pre-salt oil fields, such as Mero and Atapu. Critics argue that Shell s focus on biofuels is a mere distraction from its continued exploitation of natural resources and its disregard for environmental and human safety. In summary, Shell s biofuels project in Brazil is seen as a superficial attempt to appear environmentally conscious while the company continues its destructive practices, including investing in oil and gas projects and exploiting workers and ecosystems.
