Cisco : Three Years Of Progress On The Pathway To Net Zero

Cisco has set a net zero goal for its value chain by 2040, focusing on reducing greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions through various strategies. The company has made progress in direct operational emissions by enhancing energy efficiency and transitioning its vehicle fleet to electric vehicles (EVs). Cisco has also joined the RE100 initiative, committing to sourcing 100% renewable electricity, which supports the broader goal of increasing clean energy access. In terms of value chain emissions, Cisco aims to have 80% of its suppliers with public, absolute GHG emissions reduction targets by FY2025. As of FY23, 92% of these suppliers have set such goals. Looking ahead, Cisco is exploring new opportunities to help other organizations and regions achieve their net zero goals. These include leveraging artificial intelligence (AI) for climate change mitigation and adaptation, investing in climate technology, and accelerating the transition to clean energy. Cisco emphasizes the importance of collective efforts from individuals, businesses, and governments to achieve net zero. The company is actively collaborating with partners, customers, suppliers, and other stakeholders to turn the vision of a net zero future into reality.
