NYK Holds Annual Fleet Safety Management Meetings Fostering and Improving Awareness of Safe and Optimal Navigation by Sea and Land

Japan s largest shipbuilder, the NYK Group, has announced its annual safety management meetings for crews, employees and other companies in a bid to ensure safe operations in the sea and on the coast of Japan. The latest announcement is being released by the New York Maritime Corporation (NYK) for the first time in four years. But The NY Group says it is committed to safe and sustainable operations among all officers and staff at the firms fleet and ship-management firms in Japan and around the world, and it will continue to work together to improve their ability to cope with environmental regulations and systems, as part of an ambitious campaign to strengthen the global logistics firm, it has been revealed. Why is it so important to be known as the NHS Group? The BBC has learned that the company is working to develop new ways to tackle the impact of the oil spill in Tokyo, Japan, in what it describes as a passion for Planetary Wellbeing - and how it works to protect passengers from hazardous disasters that could cause damage to the environment and the future of maritime and land-related vessels to take advantage of its commitment to safer operations and more efficiently.
