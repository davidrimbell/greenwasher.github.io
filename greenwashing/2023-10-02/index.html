Greenpeace Urges Protection of Vulnerable Ocean Areas Amidst Labour's Support for Ban on Bottom Trawling

Published: 2023-10-02

Greenwashing, deception, misrepresentation, fraud, deceit, false advertising, whitewashing, manipulation, misleading, dishonesty, and exploitation. These are just some of the terms associated with the practice of greenwashing, a phenomenon that has become increasingly prevalent in our society. Greenwashing refers to the act of making false or misleading claims about the environmental benefits of a product, service, or company in order to appear more sustainable or eco-friendly than they actually are.

In recent news, Greenpeace has praised Labour’s support for banning bottom trawling on seamounts, but warns that the details of the ban are crucial. The organization emphasizes the need to protect the most vulnerable areas of the ocean from the most pressing threats. Greenpeace criticizes the commercial fishing industry’s proposal to protect areas that are already inaccessible to trawling while leaving biodiverse seamounts open for destruction. The organization urges the government to prioritize the protection of these vulnerable areas to allow marine life to recover and thrive.

Australia’s responsible investment market is shrinking due to pressure from super funds and regulatory crackdowns on greenwashing. The proportion of total managed funds dedicated to responsible investment decreased by 16% last year. Tighter definitions around ESG funds and strategies have contributed to this decline. The Australian Securities and Investments Commission (ASIC) has taken action against asset owners and managers for misleading stakeholders regarding ethical and responsible investment claims. Super funds are being more vigilant in reviewing their disclosures to ensure they are backed up with action.

The article also discusses the most sustainable phones available in the market, highlighting the importance of buying used phones to reduce e-waste. It emphasizes the need for businesses to focus on creating energy-efficient and sustainable data centers, as it not only benefits the environment but also provides cost efficiency and innovation opportunities. Ethical Investment Week aims to encourage people to invest ethically by helping them navigate complexities and overcome greenwashing. Food industry giants are coming together to define regenerative agriculture and address farming’s impact on climate.

However, not all news is positive. Lina Khan, the Chairwoman of the Federal Trade Commission, has gained a reputation for targeting big tech companies with antitrust actions but fails to take action against clear antitrust violations by big entities in other sectors. The SEC has announced a settlement with a major investment advisor who was found guilty of greenwashing activities, imposing a $19 million civil penalty. FortisBC has completed a pipeline to transform decomposing organic waste into renewable gas, but critics argue that the renewable gas strategy is not as environmentally friendly as claimed.

In conclusion, greenwashing continues to be a prevalent issue in our society. It is crucial for consumers to be aware of the deceptive tactics used by companies and to make informed choices. Regulatory bodies and organizations like Greenpeace play a vital role in exposing greenwashing and advocating for true sustainability. By holding companies accountable and prioritizing the protection of vulnerable areas and resources, we can work towards a more sustainable and environmentally conscious future for ourselves and future generations.
