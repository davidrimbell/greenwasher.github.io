Eni's Deceptive Practices in Nigeria Exposed, Causing Environmental Damage and Health Risks

An investigation has uncovered the deceptive practices of Italian energy company Eni, which has been responsible for oil spills in Nigeria, causing severe environmental damage. Villagers have reported that their crops have been destroyed, and Eni has failed to compensate them for the harm caused. Environmental campaigners have criticized Eni for attempting to avoid responsibility for the spills. Amnesty International has previously accused the company of providing misleading information about the severity of the spills and taking weeks to respond to reports. The investigation found that the affected areas have high levels of hydrocarbons in the soil and water, posing health risks to the community.

In the fashion industry, there is a significant problem of misrepresentation when it comes to assessing the environmental impact of clothing production. Current metrics, certification schemes, and labels used to mark the environmental consequences of fashion have been accused of greenwashing. For example, the Higg Materials Sustainability Index, a common product-labelling tool, has been suspended due to its limited assessment and failure to consider pollutants like microfibers. Other issues include unreliable data, vested interests, and a lack of standards. This lack of accurate assessments leaves consumers in the dark and perpetuates misconceptions about the environmental impact of different materials. To address this, transparency and collaboration across the industry are needed to accurately measure and reduce fashion’s environmental footprint.

Superannuation funds in Australia are being urged to commit to a 7% annual emissions reduction target to align with the country’s net-zero goals by 2050. Investors are increasing pressure on funds to cut emissions and question their short-term investments and behavior. Failure to clean up their act could result in legal consequences, similar to what happened in the UK. Super funds that fail to meet the emissions reduction target risk poor performance test results and regulatory scrutiny. Committing to the target could improve their relative returns compared to each other.
