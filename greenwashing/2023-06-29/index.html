Westport Fuel Systems and AUM Asset Management Lead the Way in Sustainability Efforts

Published: 2023-06-29

Westport Fuel Systems, a leading provider of advanced fuel delivery systems, has released its 2022 Environmental, Social and Governance (ESG) report, showcasing its commitment to sustainability and positive change. The report highlights the company’s achievements in reducing carbon emissions, promoting responsible sourcing, and prioritizing health and safety.

One of the key highlights of the report is Westport’s significant reduction of 1,498,438 tons of CO2, demonstrating its commitment to combatting climate change. The company has also made strides in waste management, with over 89% of waste being recycled. In addition, Westport has increased its consumption of solar-based energy by 55%, further reducing its carbon footprint.

Looking ahead, Westport plans to prioritize energy management initiatives and invest in renewable energy sources to continue reducing its carbon emissions. The company’s dedication to sustainability is evident in its efforts to create sustainable value and drive positive change.

In the travel retail industry, a new initiative called Project Hope (Helping Ocean Plastic Elimination) has been launched to tackle single-use plastic pollution and ocean-bound plastic waste. The initiative involves a voluntary contribution mechanism at the point of sale, allowing consumers to round up their purchases and donate the proceeds to organizations dedicated to cleaning up ocean pollution.

The goal of Project Hope is to prevent one billion plastic bottles, equivalent to 25,000 tonnes, from reaching waste streams by the end of 2024. This initiative has garnered support from stakeholders such as ACI Europe, Vienna Airport, and Cyprus Duty Free, highlighting the industry’s commitment to addressing plastic pollution and promoting sustainability.

The Federal Trade Commission (FTC) is also taking steps to address deceptive advertising claims related to environmental marketing. The FTC is reviewing and updating its Guides for the Use of Environmental Marketing Claims (Green Guides) to provide clearer guidance on labeling products as “recyclable” or having “recycled content.”

Stakeholders have expressed the need for more frequent updates to the Green Guides, clearer guidance on recyclability claims, and increased coordination between the FTC and the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA). These updates aim to prevent deceptive advertising claims and ensure that consumers have accurate information about the environmental impact of products.

In the investment management sector, AUM Asset Management Ltd. has been recognized for its environmentally sustainable approach to investing. The company has received three awards from International Business Magazine, including Best Investment Management Company Malta, Most Innovative Investment Solutions Provider Malta, and Most Sustainable Fund Manager Malta.

AUM Asset Management incorporates sustainability as a core factor in all of its investments and aims to deliver innovative investment solutions that support clients' investment and sustainability goals. The company’s recognition highlights the growing importance of ESG investing and the role of asset managers in promoting sustainability.

While there are positive developments in the realm of sustainability, it is crucial to remain vigilant against greenwashing and deceptive practices. A report by Brand Finance highlights the risks of companies misrepresenting their sustainability efforts, potentially leading to a loss of financial value.

Companies must prioritize genuine and long-term sustainability efforts to avoid reputational damage and financial losses. Transparency and accountability are key in ensuring that companies' sustainability claims align with their actual performance.

In conclusion, the fight against greenwashing and the promotion of genuine sustainability efforts are ongoing. Companies like Westport Fuel Systems and AUM Asset Management are leading the way in prioritizing sustainability and driving positive change. However, it is important for consumers to remain informed and discerning, and for regulatory bodies like the FTC to provide clear guidelines to prevent deceptive advertising claims. By working together, we can create a more sustainable future for generations to come.
