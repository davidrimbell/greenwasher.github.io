Video : Virginia Foxx Rejects White Folk Line From Democrat

In a heated exchange during a House Committee on Education and the Workforce debate on anti-ESG laws, U.S. Rep. Virginia Foxx (R-NC) reacted strongly to Rep. Sean Casten s (D-Ill) remarks about the need to address climate change and conservative America s resistance to progress. Casten referenced Martin Luther King Jr. s dream and criticized certain white individuals with low character for their resistance. Foxx, perceiving herself as targeted, demanded Casten s words be stricken from the record. The confrontation escalated as Foxx derailed the session for nearly 20 minutes before withdrawing her objection. Foxx s reaction drew attention due to her previous controversial outburst against ABC News reporter Rachel Scott, who asked Trump questions about his election fraud claims. The incident highlighted Foxx s reluctance to engage with criticism or accountability, particularly when it involves addressing the lies and hate spread by her party. The episode underscored the challenges in fostering constructive dialogue and accountability within the political arena. NewsOne published the incident, emphasizing the tension between Foxx and Casten and the broader implications of such confrontations in political discourse.
