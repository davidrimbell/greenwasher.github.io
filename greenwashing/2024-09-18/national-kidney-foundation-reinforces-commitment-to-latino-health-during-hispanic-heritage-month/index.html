National Kidney Foundation Reinforces Commitment to Latino Health During Hispanic Heritage Month

The National Kidney Foundation (NKF) is raising awareness about Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) during Hispanic Heritage Month by collaborating with Latino health professionals and celebrities. The initiative includes educational videos featuring Singer, Composer, and Producer Don Omar, and TV Journalist Pamela Silva. The videos cover topics such as the importance of early detection, resources for families affected by CKD, and practical tips for maintaining kidney health through diet. The panel discussion, moderated by Pamela Silva, addresses the specific challenges faced by the Hispanic community in terms of kidney health, including lack of health insurance, language barriers, and cultural insensitivity. It also highlights the higher prevalence of health conditions like diabetes that increase the risk of CKD. Don Omar shares his personal experience with kidney cancer to encourage Latinos to assess their risk and take steps to protect their health. The NKF also offers resources like videos about kidneys and treatment options, a mentoring program called NKF PEERS, and information on living kidney donation. The NKF s KIDNEY EQUITY FOR ALL™ mission aims to address disparities in kidney health and improve healthcare access and outcomes for underserved populations. The organization urges industry and community leaders to join them in this effort, emphasizing the importance of incorporating Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) principles into their operations. Overall, the NKF s initiative seeks to educate and empower Latino adults about the risks of CKD, promote healthy eating habits, and encourage early detection and prevention. The organization s efforts are part of a broader commitment to eliminating preventable kidney disease and dismantling structural inequities in kidney care, dialysis, and transplantation.
