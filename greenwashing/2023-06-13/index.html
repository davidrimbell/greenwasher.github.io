J.P. Morgan Launches Sustainable Investment Data Solution, Tackling Challenges in ESG Data

Published: 2023-06-13

J.P. Morgan Securities Services has launched a new solution aimed at addressing the challenges of sustainable investing data. The Sustainable Investment Data Solutions, available through Fusion by J.P. Morgan, provides institutional investors with easy access to normalized data from leading data providers. This transformative solution allows investors to extract value from sustainable investment data and offers tools for managing, screening, and creating customized metrics. With the aim of accelerating analysis, stock-selection, compliance monitoring, and reporting, J.P. Morgan aims to tackle the issues of scale, inconsistency, and incompleteness in sustainable investing data.

While some financial institutions and asset managers have been making efforts towards sustainability, they are also providing financial support to energy-intensive Bitcoin mining companies. This support is endangering global efforts to limit global warming to 1.5°C. Bitcoin mining consumes as much energy as entire countries and heavily relies on coal and natural gas plants, contributing to significant carbon emissions and pollution. Despite having environmental and climate goals, these companies continue to invest in Bitcoin mining operations that harm communities and perpetuate climate chaos. However, there is an opportunity for these companies to take action by advocating for reduced electricity consumption in Bitcoin mining and divesting from fossil fuel investments.

Investors are expressing concern about their exposure to companies with “risky” supply chains linked to deforestation. Ahead of a new European Union law that will impose fines on companies selling commodities associated with deforestation, major investors are engaging with consumer goods makers on the issue. Some investors are even considering divestment. The EU law, expected to be implemented by the end of 2024, will require companies to prove that their supply chains are not contributing to deforestation or face fines of up to 4% of their turnover. With the EU representing around 10% of global deforestation, this law aims to tackle the issue head-on.

In the mining industry, Modern Mining Technology has announced plans to go public. The company focuses on recovering gold and other valuable materials from electronic waste, providing a cleaner and more cost-effective alternative to traditional mining operations. With an IPO on the horizon, Modern Mining aims to utilize waste electronics as a resource and reduce the environmental impact of mining.

In the world of sports, the Swiss Commission for Loyalty has issued a report criticizing FIFA’s claim that the 2022 World Cup in Qatar would be carbon neutral. The report states that the claim is “false and misleading” and highlights FIFA’s inability to provide proof. The investigation was triggered by complaints from NGOs and a report by Carbon Market Watch, which found that FIFA and Qatar had used creative accounting techniques to underestimate the carbon footprint of the event. FIFA has yet to fulfill its pledge to buy enough credits to cover the tournament’s emissions.

On the corporate front, Anker Innovations, a global leader in consumer electronics, has announced its commitment to making all product packaging 100% plastic-free by 2027. The company is focusing its Corporate Social Responsibility efforts on sustainability, community, and eco-innovation. Anker Innovations has also released its first annual Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) report to disclose its sustainable development practices. The company is partnering with organizations such as Oceana and Kampgrounds of America (KOA) to support ocean conservation and provide outdoor gear to underserved youth. By using eco-friendly materials and innovative technologies, Anker Innovations aims to create more sustainable products.

These news highlights shed light on the various aspects of greenwashing and the importance of transparency and accountability in sustainability efforts. It is crucial for consumers to be aware of deceptive practices and false claims, as well as to support companies that genuinely prioritize sustainability and take concrete actions to protect the environment. By staying informed and making conscious choices, we can contribute to a greener and more sustainable future for generations to come.
