Newswire & Press Release / Nouveau Monde Graphite Selects ABB to Power Up Operations At its Matawinie Mine in Canada - Energy / Hydrogen - ABB Robotics / ABB Ltd

ABB has been contracted by Nouveau Monde Graphite to supply, construct, and commission a 120kV substation for the Phase-2 Matawinie Mine in Quebec, Canada. This project is part of the preparation for a 103,000 tons per annum graphite mining and concentrator complex. The mine, located 120 km north of Montreal, will be connected to the provincial hydropower network, providing clean energy for mining activities and concentrator production of carbon-neutral graphite concentrate for the lithium-ion battery market. Nouveau Monde Graphite aims to equip the Matawinie Mine with a zero-emission mining fleet and associated charging infrastructure, making it the world s first all-electric open-pit mine. The project is led by ABB, a global leader in mine electrification, which will oversee engineering, supply, commissioning, and start-up of the substation. ABB s solutions will optimize the mine s operations, connecting engineering know-how and software to improve manufacturing, movement, power, and operation processes. The company s Process Automation business specializes in automation, electrification, and digitalization for the process and hybrid industries. Nouveau Monde Graphite is an integrated company developing responsible mining and advanced manufacturing operations to supply the global economy with carbon-neutral active anode material for EV and renewable energy storage systems. The company is developing a fully integrated ore-to-battery-material source of graphite-based active anode material in Québec, Canada. With strong ESG standards and partnerships with anchor customers, NMG aims to become a strategic supplier to the world s leading lithium-ion battery and EV manufacturers. Overall, this project represents a significant step towards a more sustainable and resource-efficient future, with ABB and Nouveau Monde Graphite working together to make the vision of a world s first all-electric open-pit mine a reality.
