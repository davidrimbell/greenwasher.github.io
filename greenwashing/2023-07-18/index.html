Regulatory Bodies and Consumer Watchdogs Take Action to Combat Greenwashing

Published: 2023-07-18

The fight against greenwashing continues as regulatory bodies and consumer watchdogs take action to combat deceptive environmental claims made by businesses. The Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) in the UK has delayed the publication of its Sustainability Disclosure Requirements (SDR) consultation policy statement to address greenwashing. The consultation aims to introduce measures such as sustainable investment labels and disclosure requirements to tackle the issue. Quilter Cheviot has responded to these changes by launching an online training scheme to help financial advisers understand and manage greenwashing risks.

Meanwhile, the Australian Competition and Consumer Commission (ACCC) has published draft guidelines to combat greenwashing. These guidelines provide eight principles to guide businesses on acceptable practices when making environmental and sustainability claims. The ACCC conducted an internet sweep and found that 57% of businesses made concerning claims about their environmental credentials. The guidelines emphasize the need for specificity, evidence, and full disclosure in environmental claims, warning against broad, unqualified statements.

Companies across various industries are increasingly focusing on purpose-driven practices and incorporating sustainability into their business models. MIT faculty, alumni, and executives provide actionable steps and advice for managers to lead purpose-driven organizations. These steps include making operational choices that improve employee work, recognizing imperfections and taking brave actions, and balancing purpose and profit. Examples of purpose-driven practices include using fabric scraps for backpacks, providing financial literacy classes for employees, and implementing high sustainability standards for vendors.

Century Communities, a top national homebuilder, has published its 2023 Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) Report, highlighting its accomplishments in the past two years. The company aims to provide affordable housing and is exploring innovative technologies to improve construction efficiency and sustainability. The report also includes the company’s greenhouse gas emissions inventory and its efforts to address climate-related risks and opportunities.

The importance of environmental, social, and governance (ESG) performance in the food grocery retail industry is growing. Grocery retailers are focusing on reducing greenhouse gas emissions, investing in renewable energy, and implementing sustainable packaging practices. Walmart aims to achieve 100% recyclable, reusable, or compostable packaging by 2025 and reduce emissions by 35% by the same year. Kroger prioritizes food and product safety, philanthropy, and diversity in its ESG efforts, while Costco emphasizes corporate governance and risk assessment.

However, there are concerns that politically-motivated regulations and activist investment firms prioritizing ESG goals may harm everyday investors. Rep. Patrick McHenry argues that the focus should be on sound financial regulation rather than non-material environmental and social issues. He calls for getting politics out of corporate boardrooms and discouraging the weaponization of financial regulation for far-left environmental and social policies.

The European Banking Authority (EBA), European Insurance and Occupational Pensions Authority (EIOPA), and European Securities and Markets Authority (ESMA) have provided a common definition of greenwashing and outlined the risks and impacts related to greenwashing in the financial sector. The reports highlight the challenges faced by the banking, insurance, and financial markets industries in addressing greenwashing and emphasize the need for substantiated claims and reliable labeling schemes.

In other news, BlackRock, the world’s largest asset manager, has appointed Amin Nasser, CEO of Saudi Aramco, to its board of directors. This move has raised concerns among climate-conscious investors due to Saudi Aramco’s association with the fossil fuel industry. BlackRock has stated that it will remain long-term investors in carbon-intensive companies to ensure an orderly transition to a net-zero economy.

Finally, Oman is taking steps towards diversifying its energy sources and becoming a global hub for renewable energy production. The country has significant solar and wind potential, which it plans to capitalize on to produce energy and green hydrogen. This aligns with Oman’s long-term economic development framework and its goals to create new employment opportunities. ESG principles are also gaining importance in the energy sector as Oman moves towards decarbonization and a sustainable energy mix.

As the fight against greenwashing continues, regulatory bodies, consumer watchdogs, and businesses themselves are taking action to ensure transparency and accuracy in environmental and sustainability claims. It is crucial for consumers to remain vigilant and informed to make responsible choices and support businesses that genuinely prioritize sustainability.
