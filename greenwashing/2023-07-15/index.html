Roundup of Greenwashing and Sustainability: From Genuine Efforts to Deception

Published: 2023-07-15

In today’s news, we bring you a roundup of the latest developments in the world of greenwashing and sustainability. From companies making genuine efforts to deceive consumers to creative strategies aimed at shifting consumer behavior, there is much to discuss.

Let’s start with Allterrain Services (ATS) Inc, a Pan-African Facilities Services Group that believes in investing in local talent and communities. With a focus on local employment, training, and community development, ATS allocates a significant portion of its spend to support local suppliers and provide new opportunities to thousands of people. They have achieved ISO certification and boast an impressive average local employment rate of 75%. It’s heartening to see companies taking concrete steps towards sustainability and community empowerment.

On the other end of the spectrum, we have B Lab, the non-profit organization behind the B Corp certification, facing a division among its members. Some argue that major corporations should be eligible for certification, while others believe that controversial multinationals should not be held up as the gold standard in purpose-led business. B Lab is reviewing its certification requirements and proposing changes to address these concerns. It’s crucial for organizations like B Lab to strike a balance between inclusivity and maintaining the integrity of their certification.

In the retail industry, Fobi AI Inc.’s subsidiary, Passcreator, has partnered with German fashion retailer Wormland to digitalize its loyalty program. This move not only streamlines the registration process and improves customer engagement but also contributes to sustainability efforts by reducing plastic and paper consumption. Passcreator’s platform has gained traction for its digital loyalty and membership cards, showcasing how technology can play a role in promoting sustainability.

However, not all companies are genuinely committed to sustainability. Oil giant Shell is reportedly considering selling a stake in its green energy business, a move seen as an attempt to appease activist investors. Critics argue that this is more about boosting Shell’s stock market valuation than saving the planet. It’s essential for consumers to remain vigilant and hold companies accountable for their actions, ensuring that they are not engaging in greenwashing or sustainability washing.

Speaking of accountability, the climate crisis continues to escalate, with the Earth experiencing record-breaking heatwaves. Creatives are employing various strategies to shift consumer behavior towards climate-friendly choices. From leveraging gaming to raise awareness to integrating climate messages into scripts, these efforts aim to engage the public and encourage positive action. It remains to be seen if these strategies will be effective in addressing the urgency of the climate crisis.

Lastly, the beef cattle industry is facing multiple challenges, including market volatility, lack of labeling transparency, and environmental concerns. Animal rights activists and environmental extremists are posing threats, while alternative protein products, such as lab-grown meat, are gaining approval for sale. It’s crucial for cattle ranchers to take an offensive approach and advocate for the industry’s future, highlighting the safety, ethics, and environmental benefits of traditional beef production.

As concerned individuals, it is our responsibility to stay informed and discerning when it comes to greenwashing and sustainability. By supporting companies that genuinely prioritize sustainability and holding others accountable for their actions, we can contribute to a better world for future generations.
