Hanson & Doremus Investment Management Buys 8 , 793 Shares of Vanguard ESG U . S . Corporate Bond ETF ( BATS : VCEB )

Hanson & Doremus Investment Management increased its stake in Vanguard ESG U.S. Corporate Bond ETF (BATS:VCEB) by 7.4% during the second quarter, owning 127,109 shares worth $7,888,000. The ETF represents approximately 1.3% of the firm s investment portfolio, making it its 13th largest holding. Other institutional investors and hedge funds also adjusted their positions in the ETF, with New Covenant Trust Company, First Horizon Advisors Inc., Financial Gravity Asset Management Inc., American Investment Services Inc., and Equita Financial Network Inc. all buying shares during the quarter. The ETF, which tracks the Bloomberg MSCI US Corp SRI Select index, focuses on U.S. investment-grade corporate bonds with certain ESG traits. It was launched on September 22, 2020, and is managed by Vanguard. The ETF s shares opened at $64.95 on a recent Friday, with a 50-day moving average price of $63.57 and a 200-day moving average price of $62.54. Additionally, the ETF recently paid a dividend of $0.2384 to shareholders of record on September 3rd, a slight decrease from its previous dividend of $0.24. The ex-dividend date was also September 3rd.
