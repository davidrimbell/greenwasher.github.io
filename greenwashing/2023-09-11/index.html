Latest News and Updates on Sustainability and Greenwashing: Net-Zero Cruise, African Industrialization, Greenwashing Accusations, Chinese Involvement, Sustainable Investments, Corporate Governance, and Sustainable Business Trends

Published: 2023-09-11

The world of sustainability and environmental responsibility is constantly evolving, with new developments and challenges emerging every day. It can be difficult to navigate through the sea of information and understand what is truly happening in the world of greenwashing. Today, we bring you a roundup of the latest news and updates on this important topic.

First up, we have the exciting news of the world’s first net-zero greenhouse gas emissions cruise. MSC Euribia, a LNG-powered vessel, achieved this remarkable feat by using bio-LNG as fuel and applying mass balance techniques. During its maiden voyage, the ship performed 11% better than its virtual counterpart, saving 43 tons of fuel. This achievement surpasses the 2050 target for net-zero emissions cruising and sets a new standard for the industry. MSC Cruises aims to achieve net-zero emissions cruising by 2050 and is exploring the use of renewable fuels such as e-LNG, green methanol, and synthetic e-fuels.

On the African continent, Oxford Business Group (OBG) has partnered with ARISE IPP to create a video highlighting the progress of sustainable industrialization in sub-Saharan Africa. This video, called “Growth Perspectives,” showcases the success story of the Gabon Special Economic Zone and emphasizes the importance of adhering to international best practices and principles of sustainability. It serves as a valuable resource for policymakers, investors, and stakeholders interested in sustainable development strategies in Africa.

However, not all news is positive. A recent report by Market Forces has accused several Australian super funds of greenwashing. These funds have increased their investments in oil and gas companies, despite their own commitments to reduce emissions. The report highlights the need for funds to be more forceful in their engagement and divest from companies that do not align with their responsible investment strategies. It is a reminder that we must remain vigilant and hold companies accountable for their actions.

In the United States, there are concerns about the involvement of Chinese company Gotion Inc. in building electric battery plants in Michigan and Illinois. Gotion Inc., which is connected to the Chinese Communist Party (CCP), is set to receive over $1 billion in taxpayer money and incentives for these projects. The company has received grants and tax breaks from both states, raising questions about transparency and accountability. It is essential that we ensure our investments are supporting truly sustainable and responsible initiatives.

Closer to home, Counties Energy Limited has secured a significant loan to support sustainability projects and operational investment. The ASB Sustainable Transition Loan (STL) will enable the company to progress with key sustainability initiatives, such as converting its fleet to hybrid electric truck batteries. This will result in significant emissions reduction and contribute to a more sustainable future.

In the world of corporate governance, the OECD has updated its principles to include guidance on sustainability considerations. These revised principles aim to help companies navigate climate-related risks and opportunities and promote disclosure of sustainability-related information. It is a step in the right direction towards ensuring that sustainability is at the forefront of corporate decision-making.

Lastly, SEB’s new report highlights sustainable business trends, including increased growth in global clean energy investments. However, there have been declines in sustainable-linked bond and loan financing, indicating the need for further investment in sustainable initiatives. The report emphasizes the importance of ESG considerations at the company level and the need for carbon removal to achieve net-zero targets.

In conclusion, the world of greenwashing is complex and ever-changing. It is crucial that we stay informed and hold companies accountable for their actions. The news and updates we have shared today highlight both the progress and challenges in the pursuit of sustainability. Let us continue to work towards a greener and more sustainable future for ourselves and future generations.
