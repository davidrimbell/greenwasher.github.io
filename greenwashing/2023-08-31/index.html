Investment Giants Scrutinized for Reduced Support of ESG Proposals, Raises Concerns about Integrity GoodLeap Securitization Backed by $470 Million in Sustainable Loans Highlights Growing Interest in ESG Assets SAIC's Commitment to Sustainability and ESG Efforts Commendable, Demonstrates Accountability and Transparency Replacing Short Domestic Air Routes with High-Speed Trains Could Save Over 1 Million Tonnes of CO2 Emissions in Europe, Reveals Study Protests Shed Light on Deceptive Practices of APEC, Demonstrating the Power of International Solidarity Australian Government's Settlement Regarding Climate Change Risks in Bond Program a Step Forward, But More Action Needed Aramark and Amadeus Promote Sustainability and ESG Principles, Taking Steps towards a Better Future Stay Informed and Discerning to Combat Greenwashing and False Advertising, Support Genuine Sustainability Efforts

Investment giants Vanguard and BlackRock have come under scrutiny for reducing their support for ESG-related shareholder proposals during this year’s US proxy season. Both companies cited the lack of economic merit as the reason for their decreased support. This raises concerns about the integrity of their commitment to environmental, social, and governance (ESG) principles. It is important for investors to be aware of such deceptive practices and hold companies accountable for their actions.

On a more positive note, GoodLeap, America’s leading digital marketplace for sustainable solutions, has announced the closing of a securitization sponsored by Citigroup. This securitization, backed by approximately $470 million in residential solar and sustainable home improvement loans, demonstrates the growing interest in investing in high-performing ESG assets. GoodLeap’s technology platform makes it easier for financial institutions to support sustainable upgrades, empowering homeowners to reduce their carbon footprint.

Science Applications International Corp. (SAIC) has also highlighted its commitment to sustainability and ESG efforts in its fourth annual Corporate Responsibility Report. SAIC’s progress in areas such as reducing energy consumption and greenhouse gas emissions, promoting diversity and inclusion, and philanthropy is commendable. The establishment of the Office of Environmental, Social and Governance Integration further demonstrates SAIC’s dedication to accountability and transparency.

In the transportation sector, a study by Mabrian Technologies has revealed that replacing short domestic air routes with high-speed trains could save over 1 million tonnes of CO2 emissions in Europe. This highlights the potential for sustainable alternatives to reduce our carbon footprint. Additionally, PONANT’s commitment to developing a transoceanic ship with zero CO2 emissions through decarbonization technologies is a step in the right direction.

However, it is essential to remain vigilant against greenwashing and false advertising. Protests and demonstrations during the recent Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation (APEC) ministerial meetings in Seattle shed light on the deceptive practices of APEC. Activists called out APEC for promoting policies that exploit women and worsen inequality, while hypocritically promoting environmental causes. These protests demonstrate the power of international solidarity in holding organizations accountable for their actions.

In Australia, the government’s settlement with Kathleen O’Donnell regarding the disclosure of climate change risks to their bond program is a significant step. While the settlement does not explicitly acknowledge the impact of climate change on the debt program, the Treasury’s commitment to acknowledging climate change as a systemic risk is commendable. However, Australia must do more to transition to a sustainable future, as it continues to grant new mining licenses despite its commitment to net zero emissions.

Amidst these developments, companies like Aramark and Amadeus are taking steps to promote sustainability and ESG principles. Aramark’s ESG platform, Be Well. Do Well., focuses on reducing inequity, supporting communities, promoting diversity, and protecting the planet. Amadeus, on the other hand, is committed to promoting sustainable travel and has partnered with Goumbook to create the Mangrove Pledge initiative.

It is crucial for individuals to stay informed and discerning when it comes to greenwashing and false advertising. By supporting companies and initiatives that genuinely prioritize sustainability and ESG principles, we can contribute to a better future for our children and the planet.
