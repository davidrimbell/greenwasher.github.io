India Takes Action Against Greenwashing: ASCI and Department of Consumer Affairs Join Forces to Combat Deceptive Advertising

Published: 2023-06-20

In today’s news, we bring you the latest updates on the fight against greenwashing, the deceptive practice of making false pro-environment claims. The Advertising Standards Council of India (ASCI) and the department of consumer affairs are joining forces to establish a task force aimed at combating greenwashing and protecting consumers from misleading claims.

Greenwashing has become a prevalent issue in the advertising industry, with brands using terms like ‘eco-friendly’ or ‘green’ without proper verification. Such false claims not only deceive consumers but also undermine genuine efforts towards sustainability. Recognizing this, the Bureau of Indian Standards has implemented a certification scheme for green products, and the task force will work towards ensuring that brands adhere to these standards.

ASCI has already identified several examples of false green claims in various categories of advertisements. By scrutinizing these claims and educating consumers, the task force hopes to create a more transparent and responsible advertising landscape. This collaboration will also involve studying global regulations and developing guidelines on sustainability and green claims, ensuring that brands are held accountable for their actions.

In other news, we turn our attention to the efforts of the world’s largest publicly traded companies in reducing greenhouse gas emissions. Over a third of these companies have set net-zero targets, signaling a commitment to combat climate change. However, many of these targets do not meet minimum reporting standards, hindering progress towards achieving their goals.

One of the main challenges faced by these companies is the lack of accurate and reliable emissions data. Additionally, a skills gap in data analysis and difficulties in exchanging data further impede their efforts. This leads to wasted resources, compromised decision-making, and missed opportunities in the pursuit of sustainability.

To address these challenges, the implementation of a sustainability ledger is proposed. This ledger would facilitate better data management, collaboration, and integration throughout the value chain. By streamlining the reporting process and ensuring the accuracy of emissions data, companies can make informed decisions and work towards achieving their net-zero goals more effectively.

Shifting our focus to Australia, we find a significant increase in the number of Australians investing in environmentally and socially responsible funds. According to a recent report by the Australian Securities Exchange (ASX), 10.2 million adults, or 51% of the adult population, now own investments outside of superannuation or the family home. This surge in interest is driven primarily by younger investors who prioritize environmental, social, and governance (ESG) factors.

However, it is crucial for investors to thoroughly research and verify the claims made by these funds to avoid falling victim to “greenwashing.” With the growing popularity of ESG-focused investments, some funds may misrepresent their sustainability efforts, leading to deceptive practices. Investors must remain vigilant and ensure that their investments align with their values and contribute to genuine positive change.

Lastly, we turn our attention to India, where the corporate sector is set to witness a boost in sustainability reporting. The Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) has made it mandatory for the top 1,000 listed companies to report on Business Responsibility and Sustainability. This move sets an unprecedented example globally and highlights India’s commitment to sustainability.

SEBI has also proposed the inclusion of Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) disclosures for value chain partners starting in 2024-25. The Institute of Chartered Accountants of India (ICAI) has established sustainability reporting standards and collaborated with SEBI to establish the Institute of Social Auditors of India (ISAI). India is leading the way in sustainability reporting and aims to present its research during COP28.

In conclusion, the fight against greenwashing continues to gain momentum globally. With collaborations between regulatory bodies, increased investor awareness, and mandatory reporting requirements, we are moving towards a more transparent and responsible future. As consumers and investors, it is essential for us to remain vigilant, research claims thoroughly, and support genuine sustainability efforts. Together, we can combat greenwashing and create a better world for future generations.
