GUESS Releases FY 2022-2023 ESG Report Highlighting Sustainability Commitments and Progress

Published: 2023-07-06

Sustainable ecommerce is no longer just a checkbox, but a competitive advantage that drives global growth. Companies that prioritize sustainability not only benefit the environment but also see increased profitability and brand value. Adopting nature-positive strategies can potentially generate trillions of dollars in revenue and millions of jobs by 2030. To achieve a sustainable future, organizations must align their values with environmental and social responsibilities and integrate sustainability into their operations. Leveraging innovative technologies like AI, ML, and blockchain can help monitor and reduce CO2 emissions. Profitability and sustainability should go hand in hand, with a focus on meeting market demands and contributing to the betterment of the planet.

Fashion brand GUESS has released its FY 2022-2023 Environmental, Social and Governance (ESG) Report, titled “Our Best Today, Better Tomorrow.” The report highlights the company’s sustainability commitments and progress over the past nine years. GUESS conducted a double materiality assessment to identify and prioritize sustainability topics and refresh its ESG strategy. The report focuses on three impact pillars: respect nature, be a good teammate, and get things done. GUESS aims to reduce Scope 3 emissions, increase environmentally preferred materials, promote a circular economy, support women’s empowerment, prioritize employee wellness, maintain gender pay parity, and foster a diverse and inclusive workplace. The company plans to move to annual ESG reporting starting in FY2026.

Covia is committed to environmental, social, and governance (ESG) principles and offers a range of products that contribute to sustainability. Their products support workforce safety and health by minimizing airborne particles and reducing dust on construction sites. They also reduce emissions by providing locally manufactured materials, eliminating the need for long-distance transportation. Covia’s functional fillers and extenders enhance the chemical safety of paints, while their coated sand for artificial turf promotes environmental benefits such as water conservation and reduced chemical use. Covia’s business innovation program incorporates ESG principles, making it good for both the planet and their business.

The Florida Division of Bond Finance has issued a notice regarding Florida House Bill 3 (HB 3), which restricts the use of environmental, social, and corporate governance (ESG) factors in municipal debt issuances in Florida. The bill prohibits issuers from contracting with rating agencies whose ESG scores negatively impact their bond rating. However, the notice clarifies that ESG scores are an output of general credit analysis and do not independently influence credit ratings. The bill also prohibits the issuance of ESG Bonds and the use of public funds for third-party verifiers. It encourages issuers to continue disclosing material risk factors. Financial institutions are not prevented from underwriting bonds in the state.

SpartanNash and Flashfood have successfully diverted 1 million pounds of food waste and saved shoppers $1.9 million on groceries through their partnership. The collaboration began in 2020 with a pilot program and has since expanded to more stores. Flashfood allows shoppers to purchase discounted food nearing its best-by date through an app. SpartanNash is also using AI-powered technology to minimize food waste. Flashfood is expanding its partnerships with other grocery companies and now accepts SNAP benefits as payment.

Organizations can improve profitability and sustainability by effectively utilizing Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) metrics, according to experts. Sustainability is crucial for business strategy and offers opportunities for positive environmental and societal impact. However, organizations often face challenges in developing efficient and sustainable models. Decision-making is a key obstacle, as organizations must choose between environmentally friendly options that are often expensive and cheaper alternatives that harm the environment. Supply chain management plays a critical role in achieving sustainability objectives by reducing waste, energy consumption, and carbon emissions. By aligning strategies with the United Nations' goals and utilizing ESG metrics, organizations can measure progress and improve sustainability performance.

Arch Resources, Inc. has released its 2023 sustainability report, highlighting its commitment to environmental, social, and governance principles. The report includes goals and key performance indicators for 2023, as well as achievements such as a low lost-time incident rate, 100% compliance record for water quality, and significant water recycling efforts. The company also emphasizes its focus on employee safety and retention. Arch Resources is a leading producer of metallurgical products for the steel industry and is dedicated to maintaining high standards of safety and environmental stewardship. The full report can be downloaded from the company’s website.

The Malta Gaming Authority (MGA) is seeking input from its licensees on a draft voluntary Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) Code of Good Practice. The code aims to guide licensees in preparing for upcoming ESG legislation and help them align with best practices. It also aims to promote a sustainable gaming sector and improve the industry’s overall perception. The MGA is considering extending the code to the land-based sector in the future. The consultation window provides an opportunity for licensees to contribute to the development of the code.

Raven Adams, the Sustainability Manager at Granite Construction, has been recognized by the UN Global Compact Network USA as a 2023 USA SDG Pioneer. This award acknowledges her efforts in championing the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) and inspiring others to work towards the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development. Adams has led Granite Construction’s sustainability program since 2019, turning it into an industry leader in transparency, reporting, and governance. She will be honored at an awards ceremony during the UN General Assembly in September. Adams will also compete in the Global Round for the title of 2023 UN Global Compact SDG Pioneer.

Clean beauty products are gaining popularity in China, driven by environmentally conscious young consumers. The Detox Market, an American company that offers clean beauty products, recently launched in China through Alibaba Group’s Tmall Global. The clean beauty market in China is projected to be worth $87.64 billion by 2025. Sales of new brands in the clean beauty category on Tmall Global increased by 236% in 2022. The pandemic has made Chinese consumers more health-conscious, leading to the rise in demand for clean beauty products. The partnership between Tmall Global and The Detox Market allows them to reach over 1 billion customers in China.

In conclusion, sustainability and environmental responsibility are becoming increasingly important for businesses across various industries. Companies like GUESS and Covia are taking significant steps to integrate sustainability into their operations and make a positive impact on the planet. Governments are also getting involved, as seen with the Florida Division of Bond Finance’s notice regarding the use of ESG factors in municipal debt issuances. Partnerships like SpartanNash and Flashfood are finding innovative ways to reduce food waste and provide affordable options for consumers. Organizations can improve their profitability and sustainability by utilizing ESG metrics and aligning their strategies with the United Nations' goals. It is clear that sustainability is not just a trend but a necessary step towards a better future for all.
