Two Rivers special economic zone eyes tech , service firms

A Kenyan investment firm has announced plans to set up a new financial and innovation centre in the capital, Nairobi, for businesses seeking to relocate from Asian countries and offshore business process outsourcing (Bpo) overseas, according to reports from the country s leading business giant, Centum Investments (Centum Investors Group) in Kenya.. But One of the largest companies in Africa is planning to create hundreds of jobs in two Rivers, Kenya, in which millions of people are being employed by foreign workers, are to be given competitive incentives and benefit from international markets, as part of an ambitious plan to build new jobs at the centre of two rivers in south-west areas of northern the region. Companies are set to get their benefits when it comes to technology and services sectors for the first time in more than two decades, and it has been named as the best place to start the development of one of Kenyas biggest economy zones in recent years, but it is not the only place where they have gone into the business market because of its economic growth, the government has confirmed, with investors looking for an opportunity to invest in an area that is likely to attract higher revenues.
