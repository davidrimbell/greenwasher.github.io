Educational Attraction Accused of Greenwashing Over Sponsorship Deal with Citibank

Published: 2023-06-28

Educational attraction W5 in Belfast is facing accusations of greenwashing after accepting sponsorship from Citibank, the world’s second largest fossil fuel funder. Climate activists and NGOs are planning a protest outside the attraction, claiming that Citibank is “bankrolling the destruction of the Amazon and drilling in the Arctic”. The activists argue that the sponsorship deal is hypocritical given Citibank’s failure to divest from fossil fuels. W5 has responded by stating that it relies on sponsorships for its viability and development, and that it will review the concerns raised by campaigners. Citibank has defended its sponsorship, stating that it shares the goal of transitioning to a low-carbon economy.

In the construction industry, the electric truck sector is gaining traction, with companies now starting to introduce heavy electric trucks for various applications. One example is the use of electric concrete mixers, which helps meet the increasing demand for vehicles with lower noise levels and zero exhaust emissions. Aggregate Industries has successfully completed a trial of an all-electric ready-mix concrete mixer in Birmingham, while London has seen the launch of its first electric concrete mixer. These developments are part of the industry’s transition to a sustainable future and the goal of achieving net-zero emissions.

However, not all companies are as transparent in their sustainability efforts. Security researchers at Censys have discovered that hundreds of federally owned devices at 50 different agencies are exposed to the internet and potentially vulnerable to cyber attacks. These vulnerabilities violate a recently released policy by the Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA), which aims to mitigate risks associated with remotely accessible management interfaces for federal agencies. This misrepresentation of security measures puts sensitive information at risk and highlights the need for stronger cybersecurity protocols.

In the financial world, fund managers are increasingly moving away from the ESG label and instead launching thematic exchange-traded funds (ETFs) focused on specific subjects such as clean energy or gender diversity. This shift comes as the ESG label has faced political criticism and backlash. While thematic funds offer a more targeted approach, they are not guaranteed success, as some have closed this year. However, ESG funds have continued to grow, reaching $2.3 trillion in assets in Q1 2023. This manipulation of labels and investment strategies raises questions about the true commitment of fund managers to sustainable investing.

Social media influencers also face accusations of deceit and dishonesty when it comes to promoting brands. A group of American influencers faced backlash after visiting a Chinese fashion company’s warehouse accused of human rights violations and forced labor. The influencers were invited to Shein’s clothing factory to promote the brand’s working conditions and safe-labor practices. However, Shein has been accused of human rights violations and poor sustainability standards. The influencers received criticism for boosting the company’s image while ignoring the accusations against it. Shein has denied most of the allegations and stated that it abides by ESG guidelines. This kind of misleading promotion raises concerns about the authenticity of influencers and the need for greater scrutiny of the brands they endorse.

These examples highlight the prevalence of greenwashing and related deceptive practices in various industries. It is essential for consumers to be aware of these tactics and to question the true sustainability efforts of companies and organizations. By staying informed and holding businesses accountable, we can work towards a more transparent and genuinely sustainable future.
