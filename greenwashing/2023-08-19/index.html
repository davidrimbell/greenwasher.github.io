The Deceptive Practices of Corporations: From Greenwashing to Genuine Sustainability

Published: 2023-08-19

Corporations and their deceptive practices have been making headlines recently, as they engage in what some call “wokeism” while simultaneously contributing to health issues and environmental degradation. Mars and Coca-Cola are just a few examples of companies that claim to care about social causes, yet sell products that contribute to diabetes and obesity. This kind of misrepresentation and false advertising is known as greenwashing.

Greenwashing is a term used to describe the practice of companies presenting themselves as environmentally friendly or socially responsible, when in reality, their actions do not align with their claims. It is a form of deception, dishonesty, and deceit that aims to improve a company’s corporate image and appeal to socially responsible investors.

While some companies engage in greenwashing to improve their bottom line, others are taking steps towards genuine sustainability. Associated British Foods (ABF), for example, recently met with the Bangladesh Garment Manufacturers and Exporters Association (BGMEA) to discuss collaboration on sustainability and expanding business in Bangladesh. ABF praised Bangladesh’s progress in workplace safety and sustainability, highlighting the importance of environmental performance and workers' wellbeing.

However, not all companies are prioritizing sustainability. Mentions of “ESG” (environmental, social, and governance) on earnings conference calls of S&P 500 companies have been declining, indicating a shift away from left-wing ideology that affects profits. While companies may still be implementing ESG initiatives, they are being more cautious in their messaging to avoid backlash from shareholders.

On the other hand, companies like Hankook Tire are actively working towards sustainability. They have released their 2022/23 ESG Report, outlining their efforts to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and minimize environmental impact throughout their product lifecycle. Hankook Tire aims to achieve a significant reduction in greenhouse gas emissions by 2030 and is partnering with Kumho Petrochemical to develop eco-friendly tires.

The Malaysian government is also urging young people to get involved in shaping the country’s future through ESG initiatives. Embracing sustainability is no longer optional for companies, as they must comply with the Malaysian Code on Corporate Governance. Failure to do so may result in difficulty attracting talent and accessing financing.

While some companies are making genuine efforts towards sustainability, others are accused of using initiatives like the Climate Fresk workshop as a form of greenwashing. Critics argue that these workshops allow companies to appear concerned about climate change without taking substantial action. However, proponents argue that these workshops have helped them reduce financing for fossil fuels.

In conclusion, greenwashing and deceptive practices continue to be prevalent in the corporate world. While some companies are genuinely working towards sustainability, others are engaging in false advertising and misrepresentation. It is important for consumers to be aware of these practices and support companies that prioritize genuine sustainability. By holding corporations accountable, we can create a better world for future generations.
