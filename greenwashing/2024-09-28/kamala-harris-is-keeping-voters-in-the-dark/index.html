Kamala Harris is Keeping Voters in the Dark

Vice President Kamala Harris and her administration s climate policies are driving America away from a fossil-fuel society and towards a green economy. However, this transition is leaving many hardworking Americans and the global poor behind. The push for renewable energy and the implementation of radical climate policies have led to increased energy costs, with the national average price of electricity rising by 30% since January 2021. In California, where Harris endorsed policies like a cap-and-trade carbon tax, electricity prices have nearly doubled, resulting in power disconnections for tens of thousands of people. The rising energy costs are exacerbating inequality, as many working families struggle to pay their bills, cutting into their savings. The situation is even more dire in developing nations, where access to electricity is limited, and the number of people with access to electricity has decreased for the first time in decades. Despite the negative consequences of these policies, they are not inevitable. The national price of electricity only increased by 1.5% over four years under President Trump, demonstrating that alternative approaches can reduce energy costs and restore the American Dream. Policymakers have the knowledge and tools to create a more affordable and equitable energy system, but Harris s climate agenda threatens to leave millions in poverty and darkness. In conclusion, while the transition to a green economy is necessary, it must be done in a way that does not leave behind hardworking Americans and the global poor. Harris s energy plans for the next four years risk perpetuating the current crisis, and policymakers must find a balance between environmental goals and the well-being of all citizens.
