Climate Change : The New Reality - Park Record

Mark Goldfarb, a former cardiologist and resident of Park City, discusses the urgent issue of climate change during a talk at Park City Library. Drawing from Al Gore s Climate Reality training, Goldfarb emphasizes the global crisis and the need for both small and large-scale actions to mitigate its effects. Goldfarb has taken steps to reduce his carbon footprint, such as building a LEED-certified home, using an e-bike for commuting, and implementing energy-saving measures like adjusting thermostats and using cold water for laundry. He also highlights the importance of addressing climate change as a public health crisis, particularly for marginalized populations. In his talk, Goldfarb outlines various actions individuals can take to combat climate change, including using public transportation, retrofitting homes with energy-efficient appliances, and reducing single-use plastics. He also recommends larger investments like purchasing electric vehicles and installing solar panels. Goldfarb also addresses the issue of greenwashing, where major polluters spread disinformation about their environmental impact. He stresses the need for transparency and accountability in corporate actions. The talk supports Park City Library s Sustainability Resource Center, which aims to raise awareness and promote sustainable practices in the community. Goldfarb s insights and recommendations provide valuable guidance for individuals and communities to take positive action against climate change. The event is free and open to the public, with no need for advanced reservations. For more information, visit the Park City Library website or call their office.
