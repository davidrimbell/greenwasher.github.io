Investment in Anti-ESG Funds Declines as Demand Remains Low

Published: 2023-06-08

Investment in funds that oppose ESG (environmental, social, and governance) principles peaked in Q3 2021 at $376 million. However, since then, these funds have struggled to attract investors. Only 26 funds fall into this category, and most of the Q3 investments went to Strive Asset Management’s first fund. But demand for anti-ESG funds has been low, with U.S. equity funds gaining more assets.

Wood Group, an engineering company, has been accused of greenwashing. Despite receiving a £478.29m loan for a green transition, the company expanded its fossil fuel business and reduced its renewables offer. Wood Group denies the accusations and claims to remain committed to the energy transition. Critics argue that receiving a green loan should not lead to expanding fossil fuels.

Fortune Brands Innovations has published its 2022 ESG Report, highlighting its environmental, social, and governance practices. The company focuses on sustainability, safety, and wellness in its Water, Outdoors, and Security categories. It has made progress in incorporating ESG into its business, launching new sustainable and safe products, improving safety metrics, and integrating sustainability into its processes. Fortune Brands aims to improve its holistic ESG program.

VMware is committed to sustainability not only because it is the right thing to do but also as a business necessity. The company has made significant progress in reducing its carbon footprint and aims to achieve net-zero emissions by 2030. VMware’s dedicated ESG team works within the Office of the CTO to drive innovation in sustainability, such as using AI to reduce data center emissions. The company recognizes the importance of taking action and making a real impact on sustainability.

Fidelity Bank in Ghana has announced its commitment to sustainability and social impact. The bank has established a department to integrate sustainability principles into all its activities. The strategy focuses on sustainable finance, operations, and corporate social responsibility. Fidelity Bank has launched its flagship CSR initiative, Orange Impact, which supports marginalized schools. The bank also plans to reduce its carbon footprint and promote gender diversity.

Tim Nash, founder of Good Investing, emphasizes the importance of understanding clients' values and preferences in responsible investment. He suggests asking about charitable contributions and volunteering to gain insight. Nash categorizes clients into those who want to avoid companies with poor records and those who want to invest in companies that align with their values. He advises against relying too much on thematic funds and emphasizes the need for diversification.

Tokyo Electric Power Company (TEPCO) has started releasing irradiated seawater into an underwater tunnel to dispose of contaminated water from the Fukushima nuclear disaster. The process was done secretly due to protests. The tunnel will guide the water 1 kilometer offshore. The release system is almost complete, with only a reservoir remaining. The water has been used to cool damaged reactor cores since the disaster.

In conclusion, some companies face accusations of greenwashing while others, like Fortune Brands Innovations and VMware, are making progress in incorporating ESG practices. Fidelity Bank is committed to sustainability, and investors should consider their own values and preferences. Transparency and accountability are crucial, as seen in the backlash against Tokyo Electric Power Company’s secretive actions.
