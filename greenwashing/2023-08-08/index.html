Published: 2023-08-08

Seattle-based electro-desalination membrane manufacturer, Membrion, has secured $12.5 million in funding to further develop its sustainable water treatment technology. The company’s ceramic desalination membranes can recover up to 98% of water in challenging conditions, making it an eco-friendly solution for industrial and semiconductor wastewater treatment. This funding will not only support Membrion’s growth but also help lower facility operating costs, making sustainable water treatment more accessible.

In the fashion industry, UK-based apparel and gear maker, Rab, is taking a step towards transparency and sustainability with its ‘Material Facts’ program. By introducing QR codes on its clothing and sleeping bags, Rab allows consumers to access sustainability information about the product. This includes details such as the presence of harmful chemicals and the use of recycled materials. Rab’s initiative aims to empower consumers to make informed purchasing decisions and encourages other brands to adopt similar transparency measures.

MetLife, a global insurance company, has achieved carbon neutrality across its global corporate office portfolio, vehicle fleets, and employee business travel for seven consecutive years. The company is committed to reducing its consumption of energy, water, plastics, paper, and other natural resources. MetLife prioritizes sustainable building materials and design, indoor air quality, natural light, and amenities such as bike storage and healthy dining options. Despite an increase in emissions due to the return to office environments and business travel after the pandemic, MetLife’s greenhouse gas emissions have decreased by 49% since 2019. The company expects further emissions reductions as it optimizes its office real estate portfolio.

FedEx, a global shipping and logistics company, values diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI) as essential to its success. The company has implemented various programs and initiatives to recruit, retain, and develop a diverse workforce. These include career track programs, training opportunities, partnerships with organizations promoting diversity, and setting DEI objectives. FedEx also focuses on promoting DEI knowledge and engagement among its employees through learning programs, mentorship initiatives, and employee resource groups. Additionally, the company invests in communities, customers, and suppliers through financial contributions, leadership development programs, scholarships, and recruitment efforts. FedEx encourages employees to share their stories and fosters collaboration through its DEI-related resources and communication channels.

The USAID/World Council of Credit Unions' (WOCCU) Credit for Agriculture Producers' (CAP) Project and Worldwide Foundation for Credit Unions (WFCU) are supporting Ukrainian credit unions in integrating environmental, social, and governance (ESG) principles in their financial services. This initiative is in response to the importance of green reconstruction and sustainable development in Ukraine, especially in light of the ongoing conflict with Russia. The CAP Project provided training on sustainable finance principles and case-study examples of sustainable development loans. With the support of the WFCU Resilience Initiative, Ukrainian credit unions are launching a new lending program to contribute to the country’s green reconstruction.

Essential Utilities Inc., a company committed to sustainable and equitable practices, has been recognized for the second year in a row as one of Newsweek’s Most Responsible Companies in America. The company aims to operate responsibly in the communities it serves and appreciates the recognition of its efforts to meet environmental, sustainability, and corporate governance goals.

Italian marine engineering certification firm RINA has secured a significant investment to support its growth and international expansion. The private equity fund Fondo Italiano d’Investimento (FII) will take a 33% stake in RINA, while the company’s parent company, Registro Italiano Navale, will maintain a majority stake of over 50%. RINA has diversified its services over the past two decades and plans to continue expanding its digital offerings and processes in line with environmental, social, and governance (ESG) principles.

In a recent conference in Auckland, Paul Gregory, the executive director of the FMA, spoke about the harm caused by greenwashing and the challenges regulators face in addressing this issue. He emphasized the importance of clear communication and substantiation of investment approaches, as well as the need for investment managers to anticipate and make judgments about emerging incidents. Gregory also highlighted the increasing complexity of national and international standards and the need for high-quality information to help investors make informed choices.

These news highlights demonstrate the ongoing efforts of companies and organizations to promote sustainability, transparency, and responsible practices. By investing in innovative technologies, adopting transparency measures, reducing emissions, promoting diversity and inclusion, and integrating ESG principles, these entities are working towards a greener and more equitable future.
