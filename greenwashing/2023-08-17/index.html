SEC Subpoenas Asset Managers Over ESG Marketing Practices, Raising Concerns Among Investors

Published: 2023-08-17

The world of sustainable finance is facing increased scrutiny as the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) subpoenas asset managers over their environmental, social, and governance (ESG) marketing practices. This legal intervention has raised concerns among investors, especially as allegations have been made against major players like State Street and BlackRock. In the UK, the USS pension scheme successfully defended itself against a legal challenge over its investments in fossil fuels, providing reassurance to those worried about the legal risks of making ESG investment decisions.

However, not all asset owners are on the defensive. Swedish pension fund AP7 has taken a proactive approach by filing class action lawsuits against companies it believes have breached ESG norms. This demonstrates that litigation can be used as a tool to achieve ESG objectives and hold companies accountable for their actions.

In Sweden, the Norra Karr deposit holds vast reserves of rare earth elements (REE), which are crucial for renewable energy. However, the proposed open-pit mining method poses a threat to protected ecosystems and a vital freshwater source. Environmental NGOs believe that the EU Critical Raw Materials Act may simplify the permitting process for mining projects, potentially granting access to the Norra Karr deposit. This has raised concerns about the influence of industrial lobbying and the need for sustainable alternatives like energy conservation and urban mining.

The financial impacts of ignoring ESG issues are becoming more apparent to companies. Recent events such as labor agreements, extreme weather, and environmental devastation have led to financial losses for major stocks. United Parcel Service and FedEx are facing increased costs due to workers' rights and safety issues, while TUI AG, the world’s largest tour operator, lost market value after wildfires destroyed popular holiday destinations. Hawaiian Electric Industries is also facing lawsuits for exacerbating wildfires. These real-world issues highlight the importance of considering ESG factors in investment decisions.

On a more positive note, companies like Covia and Balloon Latam are working together to transform communities in Mexico. Covia has invested in initiatives to improve income, education, employment, environmental care, civic engagement, and community integration. This partnership has led to successful events like the Entrepreneurship Fair and the Resilient Jáltipan program. Covia remains committed to improving the communities in which it operates by supporting projects like fish farming, pollinator conservation, and reforestation efforts.

The shipping industry is also taking steps towards decarbonization. While the International Maritime Organisation has committed to new emission goals, the industry is still in the pilot phase of its decarbonization efforts. Carbon offsetting is a powerful tool for shipping companies to immediately reduce their net emissions. CarbonKerma offers CCUS-derived carbon credits as a marketplace for offsetting, providing quality assurance, transparency, and financial flexibility to help shipping companies meet sustainability goals.

However, not all environmental initiatives are without controversy. The extension of the Pie-IX Bus Rapid Transit (BRT) system in Montreal has faced criticism for the cutting down of over 100 mature trees. While efforts have been made to reduce the number of trees cut down, local environmental groups believe that more proactive measures should have been taken.

In the corporate world, ONEOK, Inc. and Saputo Inc. have released their annual sustainability reports, highlighting their progress and commitment to ESG performance. ONEOK has achieved reductions in greenhouse gas emissions and record safety performance, while Saputo has made commitments in areas such as food quality and safety, advancing gender balance, and responsible sourcing.

Fifo Capital has also made strides in supporting SMEs in New South Wales. The lender has appointed Brandon Robins and Sam Clarkson to strengthen its network of business partners and provide dynamic working capital solutions tailored to the unique needs of SMEs. Fifo Capital has also introduced Fifopay, a platform that helps streamline financial management for SMEs, and partnered with givvable to offer discounted cash flow loans to SMEs with sustainable or ESG credentials.

Overall, these news summaries highlight the importance of considering ESG factors in investment decisions and the ongoing challenges and opportunities in the world of sustainable finance.
