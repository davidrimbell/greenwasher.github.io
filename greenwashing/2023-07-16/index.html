Published: 2023-07-16

Auto insurers are facing significant challenges due to the rising costs of vehicle repairs, particularly for electric vehicles (EVs). A recent example involved a minor fender bender with a Rivian R1T electric pickup truck, resulting in repair costs of $42,000, almost half the starting price of the vehicle. The complexity of EV systems and the lack of an established repair network for EVs contribute to these high costs. As a result, insurance companies are experiencing significant losses and are forced to raise auto insurance rates.

The green energy transition is heavily reliant on critical minerals such as lithium, copper, and nickel. The International Energy Agency (IEA) predicts that the supply of these minerals will meet climate pledges by 2030. However, there are concerns about project delivery times, lack of geographical diversification, and progress on sustainable practices. The IEA emphasizes the need for environmental, social, and governance (ESG) improvements in the sector. While progress has been made, there is still room for improvement to achieve net zero emissions by 2050.

Climate change is having a profound impact on the real estate sector. Extreme weather conditions are affecting buildings and infrastructure projects, leading to penalties for builders who flout green norms. Environmental pollution in cities is causing people to reconsider their lifestyles and migrate to smaller cities, impacting the housing and commercial sector. The government has implemented programs and regulations to address climate change, but more steps can be taken. These include adopting eco-friendly construction technologies, incentivizing stakeholders, and implementing green financing. It is crucial for all stakeholders to work together to mitigate the impact of climate change on the real estate sector.

Greenwashing, the practice of making vague or misleading claims about a company’s environmental friendliness, remains a common issue in the market. To avoid falling for greenwashing, consumers should look for recognized certifications like B Corp, which measure a company’s social and environmental impact. Checking the recycling number on plastic packaging is also important, as only certain plastics can be recycled. Choosing companies that publish impact reports and providing feedback as a savvy consumer can help combat greenwashing. Regulatory changes, such as the European Commission’s Green Claims Directive, aim to standardize and substantiate sustainability claims.

ESG principles are increasingly recognized as essential in business strategies. The Aspen Institute’s annual ESG Summit highlighted the importance of balancing profitability with social responsibility. Companies that prioritize ESG principles can benefit from long-term societal advantages while maintaining a competitive edge. ESG has emerged as a response to societal changes such as decreasing trust in institutions and growing economic inequality. Critics argue that ESG principles distract from maximizing profits, but proponents emphasize its value in creating a sustainable and resilient economy.

Globe Telecom Inc. is taking steps to enhance its ESG performance by testing the GSMA ESG Metrics for Mobile in the Philippines. This framework aims to simplify and harmonize disclosures while promoting sustainability and responsible business practices in the mobile sector. By utilizing common industry Key Performance Indicators (KPI), Globe Telecom aims to increase consistency and transparency.

In conclusion, the challenges and opportunities surrounding greenwashing, critical minerals, climate change, and ESG principles are shaping the current landscape. It is crucial for individuals, companies, and governments to be aware of these issues and take action to ensure a sustainable and responsible future.
