Major Financial Institutions Invest Billions in Bitcoin Mining, Contradicting Climate Commitments

A new report by Greenpeace USA has revealed that major financial institutions, including BlackRock, Fidelity, Vanguard, CitiGroup, JPMorgan Chase, Goldman Sachs, Visa, Mastercard, and American Express, have collectively invested $1.5 billion in Bitcoin mining. This investment contradicts their climate commitments and the demands of science and justice.

Bitcoin is known for its high energy consumption and reliance on fossil fuels, making it a significant contributor to greenhouse gas emissions. Greenpeace USA is calling on these companies to push for changes in Bitcoin’s code to reduce its energy use and address its environmental impact.

The report also highlights the contradiction between these companies' sustainability commitments and their investments in Bitcoin. It raises questions about their true dedication to combating climate change and promoting a sustainable future.

Deposit Refundable Scheme (DRS) Announced in Goa to Address Plastic Waste

The International Institute of CSR and Sustainability, in collaboration with the Goa Chamber of Commerce and Industries, recently organized a discussion on environmental, social, and governance (ESG) issues. The event featured the announcement of the “Deposit Refundable Scheme (DRS)” by the Minister for Environment & Climate Change in Goa.

The scheme aims to find solutions to address plastic waste, a pressing environmental issue. Various industry sectors presented on integrating ESG practices into their businesses, highlighting the importance of sustainability in today’s world.

BlackRock Appoints CEO of Saudi Aramco to Its Board of Directors

BlackRock, the world’s largest asset manager, has announced that Amin Nasser, the CEO of Saudi Aramco, will be joining its board of directors. This move reflects BlackRock’s focus on the Middle East as part of its long-term strategy.

Nasser’s extensive experience in the energy industry and knowledge of the Middle East region will contribute to the board’s discussions. However, BlackRock has faced criticism for its fossil fuel investments and ESG policies. The company remains committed to providing investors with comprehensive climate data, but this appointment raises questions about its true dedication to sustainability.

CyberArk Releases ESG Report Highlighting Commitment to Sustainability

CyberArk, a leading cybersecurity company, has released its latest Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) report. The report showcases the company’s commitment to cybersecurity, business ethics, human capital management, diversity and inclusion, and environmental stewardship.

CyberArk’s achievements include SOC 3 certifications, data privacy training, a returnship program for women in technology, and expanded employee resource groups. The report aligns with industry standards and includes disclosure based on the Task Force for Climate-related Financial Disclosure framework.

Mondelēz International Releases Snacking Made Right Report

Mondelēz International, a global snacking company, has released its 2022 Snacking Made Right Report. The report highlights the company’s commitment to sustainable growth and its focus on key areas such as sustainable sourcing, reducing climate impact, promoting diversity and inclusion, and enhancing ESG governance.

The company recognizes the challenges and opportunities in the snacking industry and aims to meet the growing demand for mindful snacking. Collaboration with stakeholders is emphasized as crucial for making snacking right. The Chairman and CEO, Dirk Van de Put, expresses his pride in the company’s growth and vision for the future.

Advancements in Clean Energy Technologies Accelerate Decarbonization Process

The article discusses various developments in clean energy technologies that could accelerate the decarbonization process before 2050. These include the transportation of green ammonia, the construction of hydrogen pipelines, advancements in producing green hydrogen from seawater, developing more efficient solar cells, and the potential demand for high-temperature superconductors in compact fusion reactors.

Toyota’s commitment to solid-state batteries could also drive the growth of the electric vehicle market. These developments indicate that decarbonization may happen sooner than expected, despite criticisms of subsidies and market distortion.

Think Business, Think Hong Kong Event Showcases Business Opportunities

The Think Business, Think Hong Kong event in Bangkok received a positive response from Thai government officials and business leaders. Over 2,000 buyers from 8 ASEAN countries attended the event, showcasing their interest in using Hong Kong as a gateway for global business expansion.

The event highlighted Hong Kong’s strengths as an international business hub and featured a Hong Kong Night Cocktail Reception for networking and exploring business opportunities. Thai companies sourced new lifestyle products and services from 120 Hong Kong exhibitors, with over 80% of the offerings being new to the Thai market.

Top Ten States with Most Carbon-Intensive Economies Identified

The top ten states with the most carbon-intensive economies in 2020 and 2021 were Wyoming, West Virginia, Louisiana, North Dakota, Alaska, Kentucky, Mississippi, Indiana, Montana, and Arkansas. These states have resource-rich economies and smaller populations, contributing to their high carbon emissions per capita.

Despite the decrease in carbon dioxide emissions during the pandemic, the oil and gas sector in the United States plans to expand production, which contradicts the need to reduce emissions. Carbon offsets are also ineffective in reducing emissions, and the US has seen an increase in oil and gas exports due to the conflict in Ukraine.

Kellogg Company Commits to Creating a Better World

Kellogg Company has announced its commitment to creating a better world through its Kellogg’s Better Days® Promise strategy. By the end of 2030, Kellogg aims to create better days for 3 billion people by focusing on wellbeing, hunger, sustainability, and equity, diversity, and inclusion.

The company has achieved significant milestones, including nourishing over 989 million people, feeding 252 million people facing hunger, and reducing greenhouse gas emissions by 33%. Kellogg plans to separate into two independent companies, Kellanova and WK Kellogg Co, by the end of 2023.

Sharjah Business Women Council Offers Opportunities for Female Founders

The Sharjah Business Women Council (SBWC) is offering UAE-based female founders the opportunity to participate in Expand North Star 2023, a platform inspired by the GITEX Global tech event. Women-led startups can apply to SBWC before August 31st for a chance to be fully sponsored for the event.

Eight selected startups will have the opportunity to showcase their talent and ideas at the world’s largest startup event in Dubai. Additionally, sponsored startups will automatically enter the Supernova Challenge Pitch Competition, with a chance to win cash prizes up to $200,000. SBWC is also sponsoring the “Women in Tech” category for the sixth consecutive year.

Lab-Grown Diamond Symposium Emphasizes Value and Collaboration

Participants at the Lab-Grown Diamond Symposium in Dubai agreed that lab-grown diamonds need to find their own place in the market and not be seen as an alternative to natural diamonds. The industry must focus on creating value and differentiating lab-grown diamonds through design, creativity, and branding.

Lab-grown diamonds are expected to enter the fashion jewelry market more quickly than the engagement ring market. The symposium also highlighted the need for the lab-grown sector to broaden its focus beyond climate action and communicate other good works. The relationship between natural and lab-grown diamonds should be collaborative rather than hostile.
