Telecel Ghana Holds Sustainability Workshop for SMEs

Telecel Ghana organized an immersive workshop for SMEs, facilitated by Mckingtorch Africa, to promote sustainable business practices during the SME Month activities. The event, which included diverse entrepreneurs and employees from various SMEs, focused on integrating Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) principles to drive long-term business success. The workshop highlighted the benefits of sustainable practices, such as attracting investment and grants, and provided practical insights on waste reduction, ecological sourcing, water conservation, community engagement, energy efficiency, and upcycling. Makafui Awuku, CEO of Mckingtorch Africa, emphasized that sustainability and profitability are mutually inclusive, and SMEs can save money by incorporating sustainable practices. The hands-on activities, such as creating lip balm containers from plastic bottles and upcycling waste into art pieces, demonstrated how waste can be transformed into useful items. Participants, including representatives from Abii National Savings & Loans, expressed their intention to implement sustainable practices in their businesses. The workshop aimed to empower and strengthen the growth of emerging businesses by equipping them with knowledge and skills to adopt sustainable practices. Overall, the workshop provided valuable insights and practical solutions for SMEs to integrate sustainability into their operations, ultimately benefiting their long-term success and attracting support from global donors.
