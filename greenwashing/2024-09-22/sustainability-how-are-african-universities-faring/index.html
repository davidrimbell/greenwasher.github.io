Sustainability – How are African universities faring ?

A recent study highlights the need for African higher education institutions to bolster their efforts in climate change education and research. Despite a growing focus on sustainable development themes, such as climate change, universities must enhance their curricula, foster interdisciplinary collaboration, and build capacity to support deeper learning on these issues. The study, The role of African universities in handling climate change, examines the engagement of African universities in climate change initiatives through literature reviews, surveys, and case studies from 15 African countries. Several universities in Africa have already taken steps to address climate change through educational initiatives. For example, the University of Tlemcen in Algeria offers master s degrees in water, energy, and climate change, while the University of Botswana has created research institutes and centers to promote ecosystem services and sustainable development. The American University in Cairo has integrated climate change education into all academic programs, and the University of the Gambia is producing graduates in Education for Sustainable Development (ESD). The study emphasizes the importance of greater involvement from public authorities and private sector actors in supporting climate change education and research. However, researchers and professors in higher education institutions recognize the need for multi-stakeholder alliances and innovative learning, research, and policy guidance in environmental sustainability and green development. Despite progress in raising awareness of sustainable development education, there is still a long way to go in Africa and globally before ESD has a notable impact. The study points out that most teaching and research continue to focus on traditional ideas of competitive advantage and short-term returns for investment, rather than upholding ethical standards, responsibility, and sustainability in business decision-making. To achieve the United Nations 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development, universities in Africa need to take action plans beyond the awareness level. This includes integrating sustainability into curricula, providing specific training for administration staff, and encouraging collaboration with international research institutions on ESD research projects.
