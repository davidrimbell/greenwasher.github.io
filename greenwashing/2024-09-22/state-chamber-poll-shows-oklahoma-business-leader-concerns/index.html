State Chamber poll shows Oklahoma business leader concerns

Oklahoma business leaders favor tax cuts and incentives to attract investment and skilled workers, but prioritize improving the state s failing public education system. Despite 67% of survey respondents believing the state s economy is on the right track, a significant majority (80%) feel that students are unprepared for the workforce. The poll, conducted by the State Chamber, State Chamber Research Foundation, and Oklahoma Business Roundtable, indicates a strong preference for infrastructure and education spending over income tax cuts. Business leaders are split on remote work, with 50% expecting to increase their workforce and 37% not planning to have remote employees. On DEI policies, 73% believe they have gone too far, while 70% support leaving companies to set their own ESG policies. The poll results show a clear focus on workforce and education, with business leaders calling for pro-growth tax reform, particularly in the individual income tax. The findings present a roadmap for addressing critical areas such as taxes, workforce, and education to improve the state s business climate. Source: Tulsa World, Steve Metzer Capitol Bureau Staff Writer.
