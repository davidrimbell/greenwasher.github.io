Covia Takes Action on Climate Change, Yvon Chouinard Explores Sustainable Building, and More: Daily Green News Digest

Today’s Daily Green News Digest

In today’s news, we bring you updates on various topics related to sustainability, climate change, and corporate responsibility. We aim to provide you with a comprehensive overview of the current state of affairs, without falling into the trap of greenwashing or deceptive practices. Let’s dive in!

First up, we have Covia, a company dedicated to energy efficiency and emissions reduction. They have conducted a comprehensive assessment of their greenhouse gas emissions and are actively monitoring energy consumption in their Ottawa plant. Covia’s commitment to addressing climate change while delivering on their promises to customers is commendable. To learn more about their efforts, visit their website.

Next, we turn our attention to Yvon Chouinard, the founder of Patagonia, who is exploring sustainable building methods. Chouinard has partnered with architect Dylan Johnson to showcase the benefits of straw bale construction. This innovative approach utilizes carbon-absorbing straw as a structural material or insulation in buildings. Patagonia’s short film documenting this project highlights the potential of green building practices.

In the fashion industry, Elizabeth Cline, an expert and author, emphasizes the need for ethical and sustainable fashion that is also affordable. While acknowledging that there is no such thing as impact-free fashion, Cline advocates for a fashion industry that is sustainable, regenerative, and just. She encourages individuals to support companies making strides towards sustainability and suggests strategies such as upcycling and buying secondhand to slow down fast fashion.

Shifting our focus to the political landscape, the House Financial Services Committee (HFSC) has concluded a series of hearings on digital assets and environmental, social, and governance (ESG) legislation. Several bills were advanced, reflecting bipartisan and party-line agreement. Chairman Patrick McHenry is expected to push for full House consideration of these bills. The focus on ESG in Congress is also influenced by the upcoming rulemakings by the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) on climate risk disclosures and human capital disclosures.

In the realm of renewable energy, Ampion has launched Ampion+, a service that helps enterprise organizations reduce energy costs and meet ESG mandates. By simplifying the process of acquiring Renewable Energy Certificates (RECs), Ampion+ eliminates the need for companies to manage separate processes for energy savings and ESG efforts. This initiative demonstrates Ampion’s commitment to sustainability and corporate responsibility.

Anycolor, a company supporting Nijisanji Vtubers, has released a statement outlining its sustainability practices and actions taken to protect the Vtubers. While lacking specific details, the CEO expresses the company’s commitment to sustainable business activities. The statement also addresses cases of defamation and harassment faced by Vtubers, highlighting Anycolor’s dedication to dealing with these issues.

In India, the Arun Duggal Centre for ESG Research (CESGR) has partnered with Dun & Bradstreet to develop a data product that will assist in ESG impact assessment. This collaboration aims to address data gaps and enable accurate assessment of ESG efficiency, benefiting practitioners, investors, and policymakers. The project aligns with the European Union’s new Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive and will contribute to meeting the enhanced ESG data requirements of Indian companies.

AVEVA, a global leader in industrial software, has made significant progress towards its ESG targets for 2025. This includes a substantial reduction in emissions and advancements in gender equality. AVEVA’s commitment to sustainability has resulted in positive impacts for its customers, including cost savings and reductions in energy consumption and emissions.

Lastly, we address the issue of corporations pandering to political whims. Liberty Safe, a supposedly right-wing company, faced backlash after admitting to providing the FBI with access to a customer’s safe. This incident serves as a reminder that companies prioritize making money over political alliances, and consumers need to be aware of this reality.

That’s all for today’s news digest. We hope this overview has provided you with valuable insights into the current state of sustainability, climate change, and corporate responsibility. Stay informed, stay engaged, and together, let’s work towards a greener and more sustainable future.
