The Power of Sustainable Wealth Owners: Investing for a Better Future

Published: 2023-06-11

The Rise of Sustainable Wealth Owners and the Power of ESG Investing

The next generation of wealth owners is here, and they are bringing a fresh perspective to the world of finance. These individuals believe that wealth should be used as a tool for positive change, and they are focused on sustainable, ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance), and impact investing. They see their wealth as a means to create a more sustainable and equitable world.

For these wealth owners, sustainability, innovation, and transparency are sacred words. They prioritize organizations, investment funds, and philanthropic endeavors that reflect their values. They understand that their wealth can be a force for good, and they are committed to investing it wisely for societal impact.

But not everyone shares this vision. There are still those who view wealth purely as a means for personal enrichment, without considering the broader impact on society and the environment. These non-believers are not concerned with sustainability or transparency, and they are not interested in using their wealth to create positive change.

One area where this clash of ideologies is particularly evident is in the fossil fuel industry. While there has been an increase in the number of companies committing to net-zero emissions targets, a recent report suggests that many of these commitments are “largely meaningless.” The report found that most of these targets do not address key concerns such as Scope 3 emissions or lack transparency. Furthermore, none of the companies are making commitments to move away from fossil fuel extraction or production.

The fragrance industry, on the other hand, is starting to prioritize sustainability. Boutique fragrance houses are leading the way, incorporating sustainable practices into every aspect of their business. They are focusing on renewable packaging, minimal waste, and ethical sourcing. Some brands are even working with biotech companies to develop synthetic fragrance ingredients that mimic natural scents. These efforts are paving the way for a more ethical and less wasteful future in the fragrance industry.

Another industry that is embracing sustainability is the cannabis industry. The economic opportunity for hemp as a replacement for petroleum-based paper, packaging, and plastic is estimated to be a $53 billion business by 2030. The recent approval of hemp building materials for inclusion in the US residential building code further accelerates investment in the hemp sector. ESG-focused investors are encouraged to consider the potential of the hemp sector and its impact on the cannabis industry.

In New Zealand, the medical cannabis industry is also making strides towards sustainability. Puro New Zealand and Helius Therapeutics have won the Industry Collaboration Award for their partnership, which allows for the supply of certified organic medicinal cannabis to be grown and manufactured locally. This collaboration has not only provided Kiwi patients with greater access to locally made medicines but has also opened up opportunities for exporting the medicines overseas.

While there is progress being made in these industries, it is important to remain vigilant against greenwashing. A recent report reveals that many companies pledging to achieve net-zero greenhouse gas emissions lack credible plans to support their goals. It is crucial for companies to focus on achieving significant emissions reductions rather than relying on creative accounting to meet net-zero goals.

ESG factors are also becoming increasingly important for organizations in India. By prioritizing ESG disclosure, companies can improve their social and environmental impact while enhancing their long-term financial performance. However, challenges such as greenwashing and the lack of a comprehensive framework for ESG incorporation need to be addressed.

In conclusion, the rise of sustainable wealth owners is bringing about a shift in the way we think about wealth and its impact on society and the environment. These individuals are focused on sustainable, ESG, and impact investing, and they are using their wealth as a tool for positive change. While progress is being made in industries such as fragrance, cannabis, and medical cannabis, it is important to remain vigilant against greenwashing and ensure that companies are taking meaningful action to achieve their sustainability goals.
