Natural Disasters and Unnatural Expectations

Hurricane Helene caused widespread devastation, resulting in injuries, fatalities, and significant property loss. The impact was felt by those in its path, who faced months or years of rebuilding their lives. The disaster highlighted the increased risks due to more people living in hurricane-prone areas, with modern homes being more expensive to replace than simpler ones of the past. The role of government in disaster response is crucial, with responsibilities ranging from maintaining order to restoring infrastructure. However, political disagreements can hinder progress. The private sector, including businesses and nonprofits, also plays a significant role in recovery efforts. Insurance companies face the challenge of covering massive claims, which can be affected by the economy s management. Citizens must return to work to recover financially, but some businesses may struggle due to economic mismanagement, tax increases, and regulatory burdens. The charitable sector, while essential, faces challenges due to shifting donor priorities towards politically-directed nonprofits. This shift has led to a decrease in funding for natural disaster relief charities. The author argues that a thriving free market and a strong business community are vital for preparing for and recovering from natural disasters. The current political and economic climate, with open borders and leftist policies, has created a situation where the nation is ill-prepared for disasters. In conclusion, the author urges voters to consider which political party will support a prosperous economy and a strong business community, rather than focusing solely on their rhetoric about caring and nurturing. The author advocates for a hard-nosed businessman who can deliver a prosperous economy capable of handling the dangers of the modern world.
