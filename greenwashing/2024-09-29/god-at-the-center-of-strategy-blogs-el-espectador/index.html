God at the Center of Strategy | Blogs El Espectador

Michael Porter and Jan Rivkin s article Operational Effectiveness Is Not Strategy highlights the importance of sales and innovation in business strategy. However, the author of this text argues for a deeper layer of sustainable business success: corporate entrepreneurship, innovation, and most importantly, putting God at the center of strategy. The author s research suggests that businesses that constantly evolve, embrace new ideas, and challenge the status quo are the ones that thrive. But beyond these factors, the author believes that prioritizing God in business strategy is the ultimate foundation for true and sustainable growth. This approach is based on the idea that strong, meaningful relationships contribute to better mental health, increased longevity, and overall life satisfaction. The author emphasizes the power of sacrificial leadership, which involves making something sacred by sacrificing time, energy, or resources for the benefit of others. This approach leads to lasting relationships built on trust, respect, and genuine care. Sacrificial accompaniment is seen as an investment that yields immeasurable returns, both spiritually and materially. The author proposes a faith-driven business strategy, which involves leading with integrity, making decisions that honor God s principles, and viewing work as a way to positively impact others. This approach aligns actions with a higher purpose and recognizes that true success is measured by the positive transformations brought to the world. In conclusion, the author argues that putting God first and focusing on sacrificial leadership will lead to outcomes that are not only profitable but transformative. The best business strategy is one that puts faith and service at the heart of everything, building legacies that stand the test of time. This approach to business strategy is not only about achieving sustainable growth but also about leaving a lasting impact on society and the economy.
