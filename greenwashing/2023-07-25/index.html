Advancing China's Energy Transition: Dalian Clean Energy Group Partners with Chart Industries

Published: 2023-07-25

Dalian Clean Energy Group has partnered with Chart Industries to advance its hydrogen production project in China. This initiative integrates various sustainable technologies, including photovoltaic, energy storage, seawater desalination, and hydrogen production. By bypassing power grid limitations, the project aims to generate green electricity and produce green hydrogen, contributing to China’s energy transition journey. Chart Industries is committed to delivering safe, efficient, and eco-friendly hydrogen technologies.

KBR, a global engineering and technology company, has released its 2022 Sustainability and Corporate Responsibility Report, showcasing its progress in creating a safer and more sustainable world. The report highlights KBR’s achievements in areas such as ESG, sustainability goals, and positive societal and environmental impact. KBR’s commitment to inclusion and diversity is also emphasized through partnerships with organizations supporting underrepresented groups. The company’s efforts align with global reporting standards and frameworks, ensuring transparency and accountability.

In a significant development, the International Organization of Securities Commissions (IOSCO) has officially approved sustainability and climate disclosure standards from the International Sustainability Standards Board (ISSB). This endorsement is expected to promote consistent and comparable climate-related and sustainability-related disclosures for investors, helping to mitigate deceptive practices known as “greenwashing.” While individual countries will be responsible for adopting the standards, efforts will be made to maintain consistency. This endorsement comes at the request of influential finance leaders, highlighting the importance of transparent and accurate sustainability reporting.

The market for sustainable debt faced challenges last year, raising concerns about its ability to deliver on its promises. Transparency remains a major issue, as it is difficult to determine whether green or sustainable bonds are truly financing clean energy or achieving environmental goals. Europe is taking steps to address these concerns with a new voluntary Green Bond Standard, but some issuers may find it challenging or risky to adopt. However, sustainable bonds have an advantage over carbon offsets, as they are tied to tangible assets and have a more immediate impact on green economic activities. The market is slowly responding to investor demands for accountability, but voluntary reviews may only be sought by companies that are already performing well.

In his book “Puff Piece: How Philip Morris set vaping alight (and burned down the English language),” John Safran exposes the deceptive tactics of tobacco giant Philip Morris International (PMI). Safran highlights the absurd language used by PMI to rebrand themselves as a wellness company, such as their goal to “unsmoke” the world. However, critics, including medical professionals, express concerns about PMI’s intentions and the potential for their products to be more addictive. This serves as a reminder to remain vigilant and question the motives behind corporate messaging.

Barrick Gold Corporation, a leading mining company, emphasizes the importance of responsible mining in achieving global socio-economic development. The company’s sustainability strategy aligns with the United Nations' Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), and its recently published Sustainability Report for 2022 demonstrates how its operations contribute to specific SDGs. Barrick is committed to conforming to international best practices, including performance expectations and responsible mining principles. The company focuses on reducing greenhouse gas emissions, recycling and reusing water, and investing in local communities.

These news summaries provide a glimpse into the world of sustainability and corporate responsibility. While some companies are genuinely committed to creating a safer and more sustainable world, others engage in deceptive practices known as greenwashing. It is crucial for consumers and investors to remain informed and discerning, questioning the motives behind corporate messaging. By holding companies accountable and supporting those that prioritize transparency and genuine sustainability efforts, we can contribute to a greener and more sustainable future for generations to come.
