The Need for More Action: UN Report Highlights Insufficient Progress on Climate Change Goals

Published: 2023-09-15

A UN report on progress towards the goals of the Paris Agreement has highlighted the need for more action to mitigate climate change. The report states that global emissions have not been reduced enough to limit temperature rise to 1.5C by 2030, and calls for a scaling up of renewable energy, reforms at the local level, increased funding for green projects, and a reduction in fossil fuels and deforestation. The phasing out of fossil fuels is expected to be a key topic at the upcoming Cop28 summit in Dubai, despite opposition from oil-producing countries. Critics have raised concerns about the influence of the fossil fuel industry on the negotiations and the UAE’s human rights record.

The hospitality industry is facing increasing pressure from consumers and the corporate sector to become more eco-friendly and sustainable. Greenwashing, or promoting green initiatives without addressing negative environmental impacts, is no longer effective. Consumers are seeking brands that are genuinely committed to sustainability and are willing to spend more money on them. The industry is being asked to prove their sustainability efforts, from energy efficiency to supply chain practices. The future of sustainability in luxury hotel design includes considerations such as smart building controls, lighting optimization, renewable energy systems, improved air quality, and the use of sustainable materials.

A new report from Longview Philanthropy suggests that if the global 1 percent gave away 10 percent of their income for a year, or 2.5 percent of their net worth if their wealth outstrips their income, it would generate an additional $3.5 trillion for charity. With this amount, the report outlines several ambitious goals that could be achieved, including wiping out extreme poverty, preventing the next pandemic, ending hunger and malnutrition, providing clean water and sanitation for all, and funding contraception and maternal care. While philanthropy alone cannot solve all problems, it can play a significant role in addressing global challenges.

European Commission President Ursula von der Leyen has called for a 25% reduction in reporting obligations for small and medium businesses (SMEs) in her annual State of the Union address. This comes as new sustainability reporting obligations are set to go into effect in the EU. The European Sustainability Reporting Standards (ESRS) will standardize reporting on climate change and other ESG actions, initially applying to publicly traded and large privately held companies. The timeline for applying the standards to SMEs has been delayed, raising concerns among environmental groups. Von der Leyen’s pledge to reduce reporting obligations has alarmed activists, who question the timing and potential impact on the ESRS implementation goals. The legislation introducing the reduction is yet to be introduced, but environmental groups are expected to vigorously protect the ESRS requirements for SMEs.

Datamaran, a leading AI platform for ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) data, has unveiled its Smart ESG solution at Climate Week NYC. The company hosted an event where experts discussed the importance of insourcing ESG expertise within businesses to address the climate crisis. The event also featured a preview of the docuseries, ‘New Heroes of Sustainable Business’, which showcases C-suite leaders successfully navigating ESG challenges. The Smart ESG solution emphasizes the need for in-house ESG skills and uses technology to provide real-time data for informed decision-making. Datamaran’s platform supports companies in navigating the complex ESG landscape and prioritizing material issues.

The Social Economic Council (SER) will be hosting a symposium on October 12th, 2023, with the theme “Protect and Preserve Our Paradise, Sint Maarten’s Ecological Treasures Unveiled”. The event aims to focus on the social and environmental impact and well-being of the community. Keynote speakers include Professor Ellen van Bueren and Mr. Martyn Forde, who will address sustainable development and climate resilience. The symposium will also feature interactive sessions and an “Ask the Experts” exchange to address questions and comments from attendees. The objective is to increase awareness and gather potential solutions for sustainable urban development, biodiversity, climate change, and environmental, social, and governance (ESG) issues.

Climate demonstrations are set to take place across 50 cities in Canada as Parliament reconvenes next week. The protests coincide with the upcoming UN General Assembly session and the Climate Ambition Summit. The Global Fight to End Fossil Fuels, the organization behind the marches, has six core demands, including no new fossil fuel projects and a phase-out of existing infrastructure. Canada is one of five “Planet Wrecker” countries responsible for a majority of planned expansion in oil and gas production through 2050. The country has been urged to cap emissions from the oil and gas industry and end fossil fuel participation in climate talks.

Tens of thousands of climate activists worldwide launched protests calling for an end to the burning of fossil fuels. Led by youth-led climate groups and organizations, the protests took place in numerous countries and cities. Activists in the Philippines lay in front of government buildings, while protesters in Indonesia called for an end to dirty fuels. In Sweden, activists gathered in front of Parliament during the king’s anniversary celebrations. In the Democratic Republic of the Congo, protesters marched against corporate control of fossil fuels. The protests come as the United Nations warns that countries are not doing enough to curb global warming.

ESG (Environmental, Social, and Corporate Governance) is both an ideology and an industry that uses consumer money to impose a progressive agenda on the private sector. The ESG movement has found leverage over companies by targeting asset managers and pension funds, who have the power to vote on shareholder issues. The top three asset managers, BlackRock, Vanguard, and State Street, control a significant portion of the shares in US companies. By pressuring these asset managers, the ESG movement can influence corporate behavior. This movement is seen as anti-democratic, as it undermines the voices of consumers, farmers, and small businesses.

Salesforce has unveiled its new Net Zero Cloud technologies, which utilize AI capabilities to streamline ESG reporting for companies. The new capabilities include Einstein for Net Zero Cloud, which uses AI to suggest responses for regulatory framework criteria; the CSRD Report Builder, which automates reporting to align with Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD) requirements; and improved materiality assessment tools to identify relevant areas for businesses. These tools aim to simplify the complex landscape of ESG reporting and facilitate accurate and transparent climate-based reporting. The Einstein technology will be available in spring 2024, while the CSRD Report Builder and materiality assessment tool will be available from October 2021.
