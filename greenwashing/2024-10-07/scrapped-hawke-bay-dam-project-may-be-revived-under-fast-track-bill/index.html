Scrapped Hawke Bay dam project may be revived under fast - track bill

The Fast-track Approvals Bill could potentially revive the controversial Ruataniwha Dam project in Hawke s Bay, despite a previous Supreme Court ruling against it. The Tukituki Water Security Project aims to ensure sufficient water supply and protect rivers in the region, but faces opposition due to environmental concerns. The project, now owned by a private entity, is working on a new business case and seeks private investors rather than public funds. Environmental groups, however, argue that the project would harm native species and exacerbate water pollution and usage. Advocates for the dam believe it would solve many community problems, especially in light of climate change and water scarcity. The bill is expected to be passed into law by the end of the year. <|end|>
