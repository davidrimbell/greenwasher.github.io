Your organisation has committed to achieve net zero : Now what ?

The main conclusion from the article is that the effects of climate change are increasingly evident globally, prompting governments and organizations to commit to net zero emissions by 2050. Achieving these targets requires a comprehensive understanding of carbon footprints, implementation of emissions reduction plans, and transparent tracking of progress. The BSI Net Zero Pathway, which starts with independent carbon footprint verification and includes annual audits, is presented as a credible and comprehensive scheme to guide organizations on their journey to net zero. The article highlights the urgency of addressing climate change, as evidenced by recent extreme weather events in various parts of the world. In response, governments worldwide have set ambitious targets to limit the worst impacts and reverse environmental damage. The UK government, for instance, has committed to a 78% reduction in emissions by 2035 compared to 1990 levels. Organizations are encouraged to take action to meet these targets, but the process is complex and challenging. It involves understanding carbon footprint calculations, devising and implementing emissions reduction plans, and avoiding greenwashing. The article emphasizes the importance of credibility, which is built on trust through actions and performance. Transparency is crucial for both business success and climate action. The BSI Net Zero Barometer Report 2023 reveals that 94% of consumers consider proper verification of environmental claims important, and 73% are willing to spend more with sustainable organizations. The BSI Net Zero Pathway is presented as a solution to help organizations navigate their net zero journey. It is not a standard but an overarching scheme built on international standards and best practice guidance. The scheme starts with rigorous, independent carbon footprint verification against ISO 14064-1 requirements, ensuring accurate baselines and consideration of all relevant emissions. It also incorporates key principles from the ISO Net Zero Guidelines (IWA 42):2022, and considers planned business growth within emissions reduction plans. The article concludes by emphasizing that every organization s journey to becoming a certified net zero business will be unique. However, BSI is committed to making the process seamless and providing organizations with a clear answer
