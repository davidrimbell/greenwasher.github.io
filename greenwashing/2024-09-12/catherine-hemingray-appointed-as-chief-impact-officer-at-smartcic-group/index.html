Catherine Hemingray Appointed as Chief Impact Officer at SmartCIC Group

SmartCIC Group, a French-based company with 13 global offices, has appointed Catherine Hemingray as its Chief Impact Officer to oversee the company s culture and values as it expands. Hemingray, a co-founder of SmartCIC alongside CEO Toby Forman, will lead initiatives to support diversity and culture across the company s two businesses, SmartCIC Global Services and xvolv. The company, which serves customers in over 200 countries and territories, is committed to its core values of flexibility and agility. SmartCIC Group has demonstrated its dedication to diversity and inclusion through its partnership with Maxim Nyansa, an African NGO, which aims to provide young people in Africa with hardware, IT training, and micro-financing. In September 2024, the partnership welcomed a new class of students, with 100% women enrolled. Hemingray s role will focus on listening, empowering teams, and leading by example to ensure the company s growth is sustainable and impactful. The appointment of Hemingray comes as SmartCIC Group has experienced significant growth, with recent hires including Paula Valverde as CTIO at xvolv and Albert Bosch as Managing Director at SmartCIC Global Services. SmartCIC Group s strategy is to deliver fully flexible solutions that meet the needs of demanding and evolving environments. The company aims to help customers work smarter to achieve their objectives, with a strong commitment to culture, diversity, and sustainability. In summary, Catherine Hemingray s appointment as Chief Impact Officer at SmartCIC Group signifies the company s dedication to maintaining and scaling its business culture as it continues to grow. The company s commitment to diversity and inclusion is demonstrated through its partnership with Maxim Nyansa, and Hemingray s role will focus on empowering teams and ensuring the company s growth is sustainable and impactful.
