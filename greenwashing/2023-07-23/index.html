The Fight Against Greenwashing: New Regulations and Initiatives Promote Sustainability and Transparency

Published: 2023-07-23

In today’s rapidly changing world, businesses are facing increasing pressure to prioritize sustainability and demonstrate their commitment to environmental, social, and corporate governance (ESG) initiatives. However, there is a growing concern about the rise of greenwashing, a deceptive practice where companies misrepresent their sustainability efforts to appear more environmentally friendly than they actually are.

The European Union is taking a stand against greenwashing with the introduction of the Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD). This directive aims to hold businesses accountable for their sustainability claims by requiring them to provide detailed ESG information. Non-compliance may result in financial penalties and reputational damage. With the implementation of the CSRD, all businesses will eventually be held to these standards, ensuring transparency and preventing deceptive practices.

Meanwhile, in South Africa, the country’s energy crisis has led to a growing demand for smarter ways to use and conserve energy. The Internet of Things (IoT) technology is playing a crucial role in energy optimization, allowing for the automatic management of energy consumption and the monitoring of essential items. Companies like Vodacom are dedicated to promoting IoT adoption and fostering a digital and energy-efficient future for the country. These solutions not only optimize costs but also have environmental benefits, contributing to South Africa’s growth while addressing sustainability concerns.

However, a recent survey suggests that corporate enthusiasm for ESG initiatives may be declining. Corporate social impact teams, responsible for ESG and diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI) initiatives, are facing increased responsibilities without additional resources. This has led to longer working hours and burnout among team members. The survey also revealed low executive commitment and understanding of ESG. To ensure the success of these initiatives, there is a need for more buy-in from leadership and additional staff.

Amidst these challenges, companies like GH Commercial are leading the way in sustainability efforts. GH Commercial’s “Believe in Better” framework focuses on three pillars: “Better for People,” “Better for Planet,” and “Better for Performance.” Through initiatives such as supporting Take 3 for the Sea and achieving carbon neutrality, GH Commercial is committed to creating better interior spaces through transparency, innovation, and the use of sustainable materials.

In the Philippines, Globe has become the first GSMA member to pilot-test the GSMA ESG Metrics for Mobile. This framework aims to enhance sustainability practices and transparency in the mobile sector, aligning with international ESG reporting standards. Globe’s adoption of this framework demonstrates its commitment to sustainability and responsible business practices.

In the maritime industry, Ascenz Marorka and Greenfield Marine Technologies have announced a collaboration to support sustainability and ESG initiatives. This collaboration aims to provide a comprehensive digital package for the offshore industry, focusing on vessel performance optimization, crew safety, and environmental data analytics.

In conclusion, as businesses face increasing pressure to prioritize sustainability, it is crucial to be aware of the deceptive practice of greenwashing. The introduction of regulations like the CSRD and the adoption of frameworks like the GSMA ESG Metrics for Mobile are steps towards ensuring transparency and preventing deceptive practices. Companies like GH Commercial, Vodacom, and Globe are leading the way in sustainability efforts, demonstrating their commitment to creating a better world for future generations. By staying informed and holding businesses accountable, we can work towards a more sustainable and transparent future.
